## The recommended setup (dual eSIM)

Each phone runs **two eSIMs at once**: a cheap US line to keep your number + receive bank 2FA over Wi-Fi Calling, and a French line for local data and calls.

### US line — keep your number: **Tello** (recommended)
- **~$5/mo** per line, no contract, eSIM, runs on T-Mobile.
- Your number stays a **real carrier number**, so bank/2FA SMS (Wealthfront, BMO, trust/escrow, IRS, etc.) keeps arriving — unlike Google Voice, which many banks reject.
- **Wi-Fi Calling works from abroad** — call/text the US for free as if you were home. Tello explicitly markets this for expats.
- **Alternative:** US Mobile (~$8/mo, multi-network incl. a Verizon option, Wi-Fi Calling on all plans). Pick this if you want a Verizon-based number instead of T-Mobile.

### French line — local data: **Free Mobile** (recommended)
- **Free 5G — €19.99/mo**, no commitment, cancel anytime. Huge domestic data in France, unlimited calls/texts, plus a roaming allowance (~35 GB across 110+ destinations incl. the US) and **unlimited calls to US numbers** — handy for the pre-lease road trip through the Med/Alps.
- **Newer option: Free Max — €29.99/mo** (launched Mar 2026): *unlimited* data abroad in 118 destinations incl. the US. Only worth it if you'll be roaming heavily *outside* France; for use inside France, the €19.99 plan is plenty.
- **To keep a French number after you leave:** downgrade to the **€2/mo** plan instead of cancelling — cheap way to hold a French number for banks, CAF, Doctolib, and citizenship admin (French institutions dislike foreign numbers). *(Needs the "Travel option" enabled to use abroad.)*
- French postpaid usually needs a **French IBAN** — Mimosa (French national) can set this up; or subscribe via a Free kiosk / Fnac with a card.
- **Alternatives:** Sosh (Orange) or Bouygues B&You if Free gives trouble.

## Phase 1 — Before you fly (THIS WEEK, from US soil)

> Porting must happen while the lines are still active, and it's smoother stateside (2FA/activation work better; you can walk into a store if anything hiccups).

- [ ] **Confirm both phones are carrier-unlocked** — Settings → General → About → Carrier Lock should read "No SIM restrictions." (Verizon auto-unlocks 60 days after purchase.)
- [ ] **Check for device-payment balances** on Geoff's and Mimosa's phones — any remaining installment balance becomes **due on the final Verizon bill** when the line ports out. Know the number first.
- [ ] **Sign up with Tello** (or US Mobile) for each line, choosing **"bring your own number."** You'll need, per line: number, Verizon **account number** (`0873549945-00001`), a Verizon **Number Transfer PIN**, and billing ZIP.
- [ ] **Generate the Verizon Number Transfer PIN** (Profile → Security → Number Transfer PIN; valid 7 days).
- [ ] **Do NOT cancel Verizon manually** — the port itself closes each line. Cancelling first can lose the number.
- [ ] After each port completes (old signal drops = done), **activate the Tello eSIM** and **enable Wi-Fi Calling.**
- [ ] **Test Wi-Fi Calling on Wi-Fi:** place a call, send a text, and trigger a **bank 2FA code** to confirm it arrives.
- [ ] Note the impact on the **remaining Verizon lines** (Beverly's iPhone, iPad, watch): multi-line discounts shrink as lines leave — worth re-checking that account's pricing after these two ports (this also changes the earlier "switch to Unlimited Plus" math).
- [ ] **Keep Verizon autopay active** until the final prorated bill clears, so nothing goes to collections while you're away.

- [ ] Choose destination carrier (back to Verizon, or just stay on the cheap MVNO — many people never miss the big-carrier bill).
- [ ] **Check bring-your-own-number / switcher promos first** — the port is when you qualify for bill credits.
- [ ] From the **Tello/US Mobile dashboard**, grab per line: account number, port-out PIN, billing ZIP.
- [ ] Sign up with the new carrier → "bring your own number" → enter that info. Port completes in minutes–hours; old eSIM dies = done. Activate new eSIM, test call/text.
- [ ] Confirm the old MVNO account shows closed / no auto-renew (time it near end of a billing cycle — prepaid gives no partial refunds).
- [ ] **Free Mobile:** cancel from your *espace abonné*, **or downgrade to €2/mo** to keep a French number for future trips / citizenship admin.

## Key gotchas
- **Google Voice is not for banking numbers** — many US banks reject 2FA to VoIP. That's why the keep-alive line is a real MVNO (Tello), not GV.
- **Wi-Fi Calling depends on the network** — some hotel/office Wi-Fi blocks it. Home/apartment Wi-Fi is normally fine.
- **Don't cancel; always port** — in both directions, the port closes the old line automatically.
- **Keep the MVNO lines paid the entire time** — a lapse can permanently lose the number.
- **French number worth keeping** — €2/mo is cheap insurance for French admin if you'll be back.
