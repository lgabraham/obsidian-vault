
To love my friend is to love his questions. 

Jeff and I met 20 years ago at a question-laden time of life, University. The doors of our world were blown open by questions.  All knowledge is fallible, and we embraced the Socratic method. Above all, we wondered which questions were worth asking? What is the Good Life?

Jeff asks beautiful questions, especially for a Jock. For the past 20 years, we have asked and answered our Geoff-to-Jeff questions in the classroom, on bike rides from Texas to Alaska, during twilight hours at Burning Man, and on misty walks in the Bay. 

To love my friend is to love his questions. And if you know Jeff, you know he has many. In truth, we may have overbuilt the engine. To Jeff and me, exhilarating, critical, identity-defining. To others, exhausting. Compulsive. Without end. Sometime paralysis. 

Most of you will have chosen, naturally, an Apple or Android device. Not so for Jeff. 

You see Jeff simulates his life and business logic in excel, mastering hot keys with monk-like precision. Yet Jeff’s key relationships blossom with the blue bubbles of iMessage. Not to mention Jeff’s meticulous, Mac-based notes. All these superpowers cannot fully exist on one platform or in one device. What to do?

For nearly 10 days of his life, Jeff could not be reached or otherwise engaged because he was  hacking a Mac computer to install Microsoft excel in its native operating system. His job search was crippled for two weeks. 

Only for Jeff can the product strategies of two global technology corporations become a deeply personal existential crisis. 

Now Jeff eventually emerges from his cloud of questions. Very often his loved ones pull him through. Charles and Matt coach him. Christine offers a patient ear. The Springwater’s and now the Prills ground him with a deep sense family in two of his Cities. Spike lovingly skewers him. Misha delivers him to nature. Andres makes him laugh. Mikey pulls him out of his head with the fun stirred up by his truly irresistible charm.

We all arrive at the finish line. Jeff has answered his questions and we have answered some of our own. Our love and Jeff’s radical questioning  allow him to take mind-opening, unorthodox risks and build deep craft. All of it inspired by a work ethic he learned from his parents further forged in baseball (shout out to Ramsey). He built an entire life in Mexico City. (As an aside, for delightful surprise, watch someone from Mexico react to the Spanish that comes out of Jeff’s gringo mouth.) Then he built his career by first principles thinking of which skills to build, which companies to join and why. 

What else is arriving at who you are but deciding which questions need answered? Jeff asks beautiful questions, and his and my questions were forming our sense of self and who we wanted to be in the world. Jeff helped me answer many of my own questions, like what matters most to me, and why. 

What I have always admired about Jeff is just how fully he has wrestled with who he is. The result of soul-level questioning is a profound sense of who you are. It may not always feel this way to you the questioner, J, but I assure you it is felt and valued by all those around you. 

Jeff’s integrity and dedication are unparalleled. No mattwr the mess of questions underneath, he shows up for the people in his life with heart. He works for his team with drive that borders on mania. To be on Jeff’s team is to have his whole being in your corner, as he has been in mine. 

Until recently, perhaps the grandest question of all for Jeff remained unanswered — who would he share his life with?

Vanessa did something miraculous, which was also for her effortless because it is simply is an expression of who she is. With Vanessa, all of Jeff’s many existential questions were silent. 

Now don’t get me wrong, she is a saint for handling him. To marry my friend is to marry his questions, which will be a lifelong undertaking but I tell you as a friend of 20 years, one that continues to be one of the greater gifts of my life. Vanessa, know that Mimosa has many notes on how to managing over questioning. 

From the very beginning, when he first saw Vanessa’s smile, Jeff answered a simple yes. Later, seeing her with his friends, yes again — a natural fit, a chemistry. And I think in this Vanessa changed Jeff in a deep way, which is to teach him that there is no need to question magic when it works. 

Not only this. She expands his world by helping him better answer his own questions about who he wants to be. I know my dear Jeff does the same for you, Vanessa. 

And I will say, as I was dancing in the light of Jeff and Vanessa’s love at our beloved San Francisco Venue Public Works, I also felt that I could let go of some questions that had only moments earlier seemed so important. 

Jeff and Vanessa, I now speak to you directly. I have always thought a central mystery of great love is for two people to know each other so well, and still allow themselves to be amazed by the other. I know this with my wife, I don’t question it, even as I question everything else. 

In your life you will decide which questions to answer together and which you answer alone. There is a beautiful tension in this. Well answered together, questions bring comfort and trust. Well answered alone, they bring integrity, admiration, surprise. 

So here is to Vanessa and Jeff. May you share a lifetime of beautiful questions. And may you remember that the deepest magic in your life, which moves through you now, is known in your heart not your head and indeed defies questioning.
