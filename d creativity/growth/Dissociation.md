dissociation means that the mind and body temporarily separate experience from awareness to protect you from overwhelming emotion or threat.

Dissociation is the nervous system’s emergency brake when it believes connection or safety is lost and can’t be restored.

When connection is unpredictable or unsafe, the attachment system stays on high alert.

But constant activation is exhausting, so the body toggles between:

  

- hyperarousal (panic, protest, clinging) and
- hypoarousal (numbness, shutdown, dissociation).
