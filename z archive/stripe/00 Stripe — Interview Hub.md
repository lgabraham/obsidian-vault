## The one-line thesis (thread it through everything)

> **Selling to founders, as a founder, is my sweet spot — and I want to mold my strengths into a craft, with a best-in-class team, in a place I can call home for years.**

That single sentence answers Nicole's two real questions before she asks them: *why this seat* and *will he stay*.

## Four things to land in every answer

1. **User impact first.** Per [[z archive/stripe/misc/Habib]]: at Stripe you can't go wrong anchoring on user impact. Default move → *"I think this segment is where I can have the most impact."*
2. **The 80 is the better fit, not the consolation.** Higher velocity + more founders to help = more impact and more reps for the craft I'm here to master.
3. **Long arc.** I've done the volatility; I'm choosing depth and a home for years. (Directly defuses the boredom worry.)
4. **Answer, then stop.** Nicole flagged *verbose*. Headline first, one proof, tie to impact — then "happy to expand." See discipline rules in [[Nicole — Interview Guide]].
