# STAR Stories — Quick Cards

_Each story from the worksheet compressed to 5 bullets (Situation · Task/Obstacle · Action · Result · Learn). Glance-and-go; full versions in [[Activate Your Interviews with STAR stories - Geoff]]._

## Levi's — land & expand
- **S:** Ran an SF out-of-home ad / pedicab company; Levi's advertised outdoors but never on novel surfaces.
- **T:** Break in cold, beat the brand-alignment objection, turn it into revenue.
- **A:** Cold-walked HQ into a marketing meeting; answered the objection with our trained riders; landed a $10K pilot; parlayed it across divisions.
- **R:** $10K → $1M+ across 3 divisions and 6 buyers; still live today.
- **Learn:** Land small, prove value, multiply across the org.

## oHouse — complex cross-border deal
- **S:** My viral YC furniture marketplace; a Korean unicorn wanted US growth via a ~$20–30M acquisition.
- **T/O:** Win a foreign C-suite and board — CFO the economic buyer, CEO controlled the board.
- **A:** Nurtured the CFO ~2 yrs; flew to Korea; aligned the CEO + tech team; won the board-controlling CEO; drove toward approval.
- **R:** Reached board-approval stage end-to-end; paused only when tariffs hit the supply chain.
- **Learn:** De-risk macro exposure earlier — but I can carry a complex deal to the decision maker.

## SFMTA — influence without authority
- **S:** Pedicab company blocked by agencies' conflicting reading of the law.
- **T/O:** Get regulatory clarity to expand into Moscone and the Marina.
- **A:** Engaged SFMTA, SF Port, the City, and SFPD; built a shared understanding; negotiated expanded zones; documented it industry-wide.
- **R:** Industry-wide clarity; protected the business; opened a $2M+ conference-activation market.
- **Learn:** Influence without authority = align everyone on a shared truth.

## Reddit — deliver outstanding results
- **S:** YC pivot to a furniture idea resonating on Reddit; partner challenged us to 100K visitors by Demo Day.
- **T/O:** Massive top-of-funnel, fast, no budget.
- **A:** Weeks of community + message-craft; tested angles until a post hit the Reddit homepage; made it travel.
- **R:** 100K by Demo Day; an influencer picked it up → 13M views.
- **Learn:** Community + message beat spend; set a number and own it.

## $15M model — limited resources / analytics
- **S:** Advise Phonely (YC voice-AI) raising $15M with no revenue handle or forecast.
- **T:** Credible financials and narrative, fast.
- **A:** Dug into raw data; built a bottom-up model in <2 weeks; split revenue types; coached the CEO; surfaced unused analytics.
- **R:** Powered the $15M raise; founder got a handle on the business for the first time.
- **Learn:** Founders underuse their own data — meeting them there is my edge (and Stripe's thesis).

## Wind-down — accountability
- **S:** Had to wind down a company.
- **T/O:** Make the call, protect team and investors, keep trust intact.
- **A:** Made the decision; gave early honest notice; called every investor; stayed to the end; helped the team land.
- **R:** Team called it their best job and offered to work for free; investors offered to back the next thing.
- **Learn:** How you end something determines who backs you next.

## 120 weeks of user interviews — users first
- **S:** Furniture marketplace; buyers had no sane way to shop.
- **T:** Keep real user needs central, not assumptions.
- **A:** 1–2 interviews/week for ~120 weeks; synthesized the pain; played the best one to the whole team; let it drive the roadmap.
- **R:** Users at the center for 2+ years; fueled growth and messaging.
- **Learn:** Listening cadence is a discipline; firsthand beats a summary.

## The Explorer Fund — velocity, sourcing & winning with help _(closest analog to the role)_
- **S:** Cofounded a $4M seed fund (Rish, Marcin, Andrew), Eric Schmidt-backed; as a small fund we competed for allocations against far bigger VCs.
- **T:** Get into the best startups at the earliest, most competitive stage — no brand, small checks, high volume.
- **A:** Built a batch-segmented sourcing engine across my YC + GSB networks; formed early views on the best teams; won access by offering help as a founder; prioritized ruthlessly and ran rapid diligence.
- **R:** Backed 30+ companies over ~6 yrs (~5x up, ~2x returned); first check on a deal later marked up by a16z.
- **Learn:** At velocity, a segmented system + leading with help beats brand and budget — exactly how I'd run a book of 80.

## Stanford — resilience / handling a "no"
- **S:** Applied to Stanford GSB; rejected.
- **T/O:** Treat the "no" as a setup, not an end.
- **A:** Kept scaling the business; landed Levi's Super Bowl 50; got the Levi's president's rec; reapplied; stayed engaged on the waitlist.
- **R:** Admitted as 1 of 10 off a 1,000-person waitlist; rejection letter on my wall.
- **Learn:** A "no" is information — build your way back.
