# Nicole — Drills

_Habib's idea: rehearse **sub-60-second** answers to the questions Nicole is likely to ask, and nail them. These are cut tight — aim for **~30 seconds, 2–3 sentences.** Land the headline, stop, and offer to expand. See [[Nicole — Interview Guide]] for the why._

> **Format:** 🎯 = the one thing to land · ⛔ = the trap.

---

### 1. "Tell me about yourself."
🎯 *Founder choosing to master selling to founders. Warm, fast, stop.*

Three-time founder — my ventures did $25M+ in sales, and I've angel-invested in 25+ startups now doing $100M+. My whole career I've listened deeply, given voice to the moment, and solved problems. I've loved founding, but I want to mold those into one craft, with a best-in-class team. Selling to founders, as a founder, is my sweet spot.

⛔ Don't recite the résumé. Land the thesis and stop.

---

### 2. "Why this role / why this team?"
🎯 *User impact + I identify with founders.*

Two reasons. Impact — startups badly underutilize Stripe, and this high-volume segment is where I can move the needle for the most founders. And fit — these are founder-run platforms, the group I identify with; selling to them as a founder is where I'm most useful.

⛔ Don't make it about prestige or comp. Anchor on the user.

---

### 3. "You first interviewed for the other team — ~15 accounts. This is ~80. Why the switch?"
🎯 *80 is the better fit, not the consolation.*

Honestly, 80 is the better fit, not the consolation. The first team was fewer, more mature accounts; this is higher-velocity platforms, closer to the founders I know. I run best with many balls in the air — founder muscle — and more accounts means more founders I can help.

⛔ Never disparage the other team or sound like you're settling.

---

### 4. "Won't you get bored in two months?"
🎯 *Name the worry; flip it — velocity is the antidote.*

I get the worry — but what bores me is a narrow, static book, not volume. Eighty fast-moving platforms is the opposite: constant variety, my own book to run. I'm here to master a craft, so I want the reps — and I've done the volatility; I'm choosing depth and a home for years.

⛔ Don't get defensive or over-explain. Say it once, with conviction.

---

### 5. "Why sales, why now?"
🎯 *Deliberate choice to master a craft — not a fallback.*

A deliberate choice, not a fallback. Across three companies the part I loved most was being in front of people — finding the message that lands, moving a deal; I've sold since I was 16. What I haven't done is master it in a world-class org with real coaching. Now I want to go deep on one craft.

⛔ Don't lead with "escaping founder volatility." Lead with the pull.

---

### 6. "Why Stripe?"
🎯 *Three crisp observations, then stop.*

Three things became obvious over five years near YC. One, how essential payments data is — and how little founders use it. Two, Stripe's pride in craft; nothing I've used matches its precision. Three, how badly startups underutilize the suite. So it's where I can hone selling, to a group I love, and stay for years.

⛔ Don't open by gushing about the Collisons. Lead with the three observations.

---

### 7. "What's your 3–5 year vision?"
🎯 *Master the craft → GTM leadership. Long game.*

Near term, be exceptional at the craft — top of the team on fundamentals and fluent in Connect. Over three to five years, toward go-to-market leadership, the Stripe way, once earned. Clear on where I'm going, flexible on how — optimizing for depth and a long arc, not the next jump.

⛔ Never hint at founding again. "Long arc," "a home," "the next jump" do the work.

---

### 8. "How would you approach a book of 80 accounts?"
🎯 *Founder-operator: segment by potential, get to the why, systematize.*

Like a founder runs a company — find the leverage. I'd segment the 80 by potential: volume, trajectory, how much of the platform they underutilize. Concentrate where I can drive the most expansion, get to the why behind each business, systematize the rest. The playbook: more volume, more products, embedded finance.

⛔ Don't say you'll treat all 80 equally. Show prioritization + the levers.

---

### 9. "Where will you need to grow? / What worries you?"
🎯 *Honest: beginner on mechanics, fast ramp.*

I'm strong on outcomes and relationships; I'm a beginner on Stripe mechanics — Connect, your tooling, your motion. I won't pretend otherwise, and I'm here to be coached. My track record says I ramp fast: a bottom-up model in two weeks, cold into Levi's HQ.

⛔ Don't pick a fake weakness. Name the real one, pair it with fast-ramp proof.
