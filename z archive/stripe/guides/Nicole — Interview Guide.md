# Nicole — Interview Guide

_The tallest task. Nicole is James' manager; she gets a say in her future team. This is a **fit** conversation, not a gauntlet._ → drill the answers in [[Nicole — Drills]].

## What she's really testing

1. **Why am I a fit for *this* role** — the ~80-account platform team — *after* I interviewed for the other team (fewer, ~15 more-mature accounts)? She needs to believe this seat is the one I actually want, not a backup I accepted.
2. **Will I get bored in two months?** The unspoken founder worry: restless, flight risk.
3. **Can I be concise?** Her note on me said **verbose**. How I answer *is* part of what she's evaluating. Tight answers are the proof.

Underneath all three is one question: **is this a person who wants this exact seat, and will stay and thrive in it?**

## The thesis (say it early, in my words)

> I've loved founding, but I feel called to mold my natural strengths — listening deeply, giving voice to the moment, solving problems — into one craft, with a best-in-class team. **Selling to founders, as a founder, is my sweet spot.** This is where I can have the most impact, and it's somewhere I want to call home for years.

## The 15 → 80 reframe (the heart of it)

Don't defend the switch — **claim it as the better fit.**

- The first team was **fewer relationships with more mature businesses**. This team is **higher-velocity platforms, earlier in their arc** — b2b2c, founder-run, closer to the gap I've seen firsthand.
- **More surface area = more founders I can actually help.** I've watched startups massively underutilize Stripe (the revenue data alone is rich with insight they never touch). 80 accounts means I move the needle for *more* of them. → *"This segment is where I can have the most impact."*
- **It's the group I identify with and love.** I've invested alongside top VCs by being a founder who helps founders. Selling to them as a founder is where I'm most credible and most useful.
- **It suits how I'm wired.** I run best with many balls in the air and my own book to run — *founder of your own book*. That's founder muscle, not a stretch.
- The first process **only grew my respect** for Nicole, Cassius, and the team. This is the seat I want, not the seat I settled for.

## The "won't get bored" answer

Name it, then flip it. Don't get defensive.

- **What bores me is narrow, slow, and static** — not volume. Eighty fast-moving platforms is the *opposite*: constant variety, always another problem, my own book.
- **I'm here to master a craft**, which means I *want* the reps. Boredom shows up when there's nothing left to learn; I'm at the very start of that curve.
- **Roots.** I've got a family now and I've done the founder volatility. I'm deliberately choosing depth and a place I can stay — *a home for years to come*. (More deliberate, not less hungry.)

## User-impact anchor (Habib's rule)

Per [[z archive/stripe/misc/Habib]], at Stripe **you can't go wrong anchoring on user impact.** When unsure where to take any answer, route it back to the founder/user: their underused data, their growth, the value they're leaving on the table. Default line: *"I think this segment is where I can have the most impact."*

## Verbosity discipline (this is graded)

- **One-breath rule:** lead with the headline sentence, then stop.
- **Three beats max:** claim → one proof → tie to impact. Land it.
- **Answer, then offer:** end with *"Happy to expand on any of that"* instead of expanding unprompted.
- **Catch the spiral:** if you hear yourself say "and another thing…", you've already passed the landing.
- **Practice against a timer.** Every drill in [[Nicole — Drills]] is built to come in **under 60 seconds.**

## 3–5 year vision (have it crisp)

Master the craft now → **go-to-market leadership** over 3–5 years, the Stripe way, once earned. *Clear on where I'm going, flexible on how I get there.* Optimizing for depth and a long arc, not the next jump.

## Questions to ask Nicole

- What have you seen as the **early signals of success** for someone in this seat? _(from [[Nicole]])_
- As you build this team, what does a great **first 6–12 months** look like?
- What separates the people who **hit critical velocity fast** from those who don't?
- How do you **develop and coach** people once they're ramped?
