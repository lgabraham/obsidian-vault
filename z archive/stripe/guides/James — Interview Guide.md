# James — Interview Guide

_Second meeting with James Matheson, the hiring manager. The first was relationship/soft-sell (2× Olympian, AE → manager fast, coachable/grind). **This round he tests practical, on-the-job skill** — can I actually do the work of running a platform book._

## Frame
He went **AE → manager fast** and has run platform/marketplace accounts himself. He's listening for someone who **thinks like an operator and a seller**, not just tells good stories. Show judgment, structure, and that I'd be low-maintenance to manage. Speak his language: **coaching, reps, performing under pressure** (the moguls operating system).

## Scenario 1 — The escalation (most likely; from [[z archive/stripe/misc/Habib]])
**Setup:** "Things are going well, you're on the job. A user emails your manager (Patrick) saying they were misled — and there's no paper trail of the timeline change. What do you do?"

**Playbook — say it in this order:**
1. **Truth-finding first.** Understand what actually happened, the user's intent, and the real goal underneath the complaint — before reacting or defending.
2. **Balance Stripe and the user.** Own the relationship; don't throw a colleague under the bus or blindly concede.
3. **Set Patrick up to win.** Draft a **ghost note** for him to send, with talking points — *eat the pain to set others up for success.*
4. **Escalate cleanly.** Loop in the head of org with talking points and an **execution plan**, so they're informed, not surprised.
5. **Close the loop + prevent recurrence.** Have Patrick send a follow-up **ghost note in ~2 months** to check in, and **change the process** (document timeline changes) so it can't happen again.

🎯 Land: *truth-finding → protect the user and the team → systemic fix.*

## Scenario 2 — "A time you had to do too much with limited resources"
Use a real one, tight (STAR, weight the Action):
- **$15M raise / bottom-up model in 2 weeks** — arrived to no handle on revenue or forecast; coached the CEO; built the model fast. _(meticulous, fast, end-to-end)_
- **Reddit → 100K by Demo Day** — community + message-craft to the Reddit homepage; then 13M influencer views. _(scrappy, results)_
- **Wind-down** — made the call, gave notice, called every investor, went down with the ship. _(accountable under pressure)_

## Scenario 3 — Mock discovery / role-play (be ready for a live fictional B2B)
Structure the call, then give the exec the choice of topic:
1. **Set the agenda:** *"I'd love X min on your business, Y on where payments fit, and leave the last few for next steps."*
2. **Open wide:** *"What's happening with the business overall — the exciting milestones and priorities for this next phase?"*
3. **Then payments:** *"How does payments fit today, and how would you want it to fit going forward?"*
4. **Map to value:** land on the three expansion levers — **process more volume → sell more products → embedded finance.** _(Connect detail lives in [[Technical — Connect Guide]].)_

⛔ Don't jump straight to strategy/product. Get to the **why** first (curiosity is the #1 success signal Nicole/Cassius named).

## Scenario 4 — "How would you run your book of 80?"
Same as the [[Nicole — Drills|prioritization drill #8]]: segment by potential (volume, trajectory, underutilization), concentrate on the high-leverage accounts, systematize the rest, relentlessly get to the *why*. **Founder of your own book.**

## What James is grading
- **Judgment under ambiguity** — do I find the truth before reacting?
- **Operator instinct** — structure, prioritization, follow-through.
- **Coachability** — I take the input and run; beginner's mind on Stripe mechanics.
- **Manageability** — would he have to babysit me? No.

## Questions to ask James
- What does a great **first 6–12 months** look like in this seat?
- What's the **coaching cadence** once someone's ramped — how do you develop people?
- _(if rapport's warm)_ The discipline of moguls is brutal repetition under judging pressure — **what carried over** for you into sales?
