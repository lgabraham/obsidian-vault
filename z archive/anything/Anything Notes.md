
**Summary** 

After looking through 25 users who pay us or make money (linked here) I flagged the users into groups. There are surprisingly global, which makes messaging interesting.

* Creativity motivated -- ideates through many ideas
* Idea motivated -- tries to bring a single idea to life (sometimes compulsively => churn risk)
* Business motivated -- making apps for others, possibly as a service

I count "money-motivated" may be a subset of idea motivated. I did find one user who may be "Anything profitable". His app suggests people commit money to build habits (myprocessapp.com). He has paid $481 to Anything, and made $1,907.86. Sick app.

**Finding simple, aligned language of the benefit in user terms**

One thing I've been confused about for myself is how to align marketing language with the target demographic, who seems to (so far) mostly non-technical. As I understand the market, we could choose to speak to increasingly technical talent who would evolve deeper into our platform (Replit) or speak to non-technical folks (Lovable) who are "vibe coding".

I wonder how much technical language vibe coders understand. For example, I did not know what an "app" is until fairly recently, even as a tech founder. As the team leans technical, language that is legible to y'all may not be to others. 

Other examples of technical words on the main page: app, web app, mobile app, Postgres database, 100k+ lines of code, dev environment, production environment, refactor, deploy, ship, config, keys, integrations, production

The question I keep asking myself is: how do these users understand the benefit, and how do we speak to them in language they understand?

"All apps come with an instant dev and production Postgres database. No set up needed and 1GB+ free per app"

"Every project comes ready to store and organize your data — safely and instantly, no setup required."

To be clear, there is lots of simple language that is easy to understand.

**Is creativity end to itself?**

While making money surely is exciting -- possibly viral -- it also seems like a high expectation to set for users. Only 2 of 500,000 users may be doing this. Whereas many are succeeding at being successful and seemingly having a great time. I wonder if self-expression in software is exciting enough itself.

Here is some simple hero language I've thought about for tapping into aspirational creativity without mentioning money.
* Your imagination, made real.
* The power of software at your fingertips.
* Experiment without limits.

If we were to mention money, I could imagine language like this:
* Turn your idea into income online.
