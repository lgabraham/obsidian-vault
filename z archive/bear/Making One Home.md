# Making One Home

Between us, Dane and I have officiated 9 weddings. 

An enormous honor. If I have any idea what love is or feels like, it is both because I was well loved, and because I worked very hard at expressing love. 

Having a point of view on what love is is also frightening. Used to be the role of a church, institution with years of knowledge. What do I know?

Visual metaphors help. A fire that needs to be fed. Two trees that grow together. Making one home. 

A house is not a home. 

Even when you are at home by yourself, you are not alone. 

Speaks to the work — a choice made every day — to choose the family. 

Our ancestors have been making homes for far longer than they believed in any one god or many gods. It has always been a matter of survival and a matter of love. 

Tattoo?

You can make a home with anyone. The commitment to make one home lasts until one of you dies. It is very hard. 

On its own, a shelter, a roof over your head, is not a home. Countless small acts of loving ceremony make a shelter into a home. You do not make a home by yourself. Even when you are alone, and are at home, you are linked to your family and your ancestors by things you do or by relics that you honor.
