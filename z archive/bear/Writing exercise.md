# Writing exercise 

Write 4 times 4 days in a row, or over 4 weeks about a traumatic event

Write about the same event for all 4 episodes for at least 15-30 min. 

Write continuously. 

Option: analyze by circling negative words and squaring positive words. 

Include: 
Facts about who was there / what happens
Facts about how it made you feel then / now
Any links or associations between this and anything at all past present and future

The nervous system is a prediction machine 

Childhood is a critical period of building this machine. 

This writing taps into your “map” or “schema”

A hallmark of trauma is that our brain or nervous system is less engaged than it would be in the healthy condition. 

The repeated visiting of traumatic events, in a structured way — and structure and coherence increases with successive writings — increases the amount of truth telling.

Thinking about the human as a universal computer and the nature of “state” ie fear
