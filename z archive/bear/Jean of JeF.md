# Jean of JeF
JeF,

My name is Lawrence Geoffrey Abraham. My Dad’s was Lawrence Patrick Abraham. My Grandfather’s was Lawrence Thomas Abraham. 

For a long time, I wondered whether my child would have any of my father’s names. I may have even get it was expect of me. It wasn’t until recently in my life I knew I wanted my children to have names of their own. 

When I first began falling in love with Mimosa, I encountered you in my life, and you broke all my models of masculinity. 

Where my forefathers defined masculinity with a kind machismo, you defined yours in a totally different manner. 

In place of grand gestures, you showed free expression. In place of control, you showed an open mind. In place of control, you showed love and curiosity. You were secure unto yourself. 

And yet all of the things I valued about the men in my life you retained. Strength, competence, strong views. You are a foundation pillar of your family. 

And then there is your bond with Mimosa,  itself, which is itself beautiful. Her courage and adventure, which I admire and love, come from you. 

She once told me that every time she jumps off a cliff into water she can still here you say “C’mon, Mosa”

The woman that I love is her own person, no doubt. And she is who she is because you were her father. You loved her well, and so she is magnificent. 

If my Naya Jean becomes anything like her mother — anything like you — I will be glad. 

In loving her, I myself changed. I discovered my own ideas of masculinity, informed in part by your brilliant example. 

I think it is poetic that rather than have a boy with the name Lawrence — I don’t intend to, but I might have if not for meeting Mimosa — I will have a girl with the name Jean. 

I am so happy that Naya will get to meet her namesake — to have you show her the world. There is so much to show her, isn’t there?

Love,
Geoff
