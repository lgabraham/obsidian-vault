# Deutsch / Woodruff / Maslow

**1. Introduction: Why Ethics Needs Physics (and Vice Versa)**
→ Introduce the motivation for this fusion: ethics as more than rules, physics as more than description—both are about reality *and* becoming.

**2. Knowledge, Creation, and the Fabric of Reality**
→ Introduce Deutschian ideas: explanatory knowledge, constructor theory, universality.
→ Introduce the notion that persons are knowledge-bearing constructors.
**Possible subheading:** *Selfhood as a Knowledge Process*

**3. Reverence and the Limits of Knowing**
→ Enter Paul Woodruff: reverence, humility, and moral limits.
→ Contrast with physics’ drive to explain; reveal tension and harmony.
**Possible addition:** Explore reverence not as anti-knowledge, but as what guides *the use* of knowledge.

**4. The Self as a Moral Constructor** ← *This is your home base for actualization*
→ **New section proposal:**
* Self-actualization as the process of becoming a moral constructor
* Actualization as explanatory knowledge of the self
* The virtues (reverence, courage, creativity) as *tests of good explanations*
* Peak experiences and growth as knowledge-generation events
→ Use quotes from Maslow, Deutsch, and Woodruff
→ Frame this as a bridge between ethics and epistemology **within the person**

**5. Relationships as Joint Constructions of Meaning**
→ Extend actualization into shared domains: love, institutions, legacy
→ Knowledge and reverence become co-created
→ May include parenting, partnership, citizenship

**6. Institutions and the Error-Correcting Soul**
→ Build on Deutsch’s ideas of error correction and link to Woodruff’s reverence for tradition and democratic institutions
→ Institutions as expressions of both actualized selves and *limits* on self

**7. Toward an Open Future**
→ Final reflections on living as both an agent of explanation and an ethical being
→ Actualization reframed as *never complete*, always iterative—like science, like virtue
