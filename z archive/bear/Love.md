# Love

Micah and Tatiana met at the Sub Dog, where they both, loving books, felt at home. 

Micah remembers speaking about Shakespeare. Tatiana remembers Micah hovering awkwardly around a conversation about Shakespeare she was having with a friend. She then discovered Micah made surprisingly substantial points. After he walked into the bookstore 3 nights in a row, she realized that Micah lived here. She did not know people her age lived there. 

Early in lockdown, Tatiana and Micah began meeting for walks and runs. Tatiana counted Micah a true friend, as she made friends for life. 

When we asked Micah what he loved about Tatiana, he looked truly flabbergasted. Where to begin?

Micah deeply admires Tatiana’s intelligence and her competence at many things. She is playful, conscientious, fiercely loyal. 

She also has beautiful hands. 

Micah is in awe of Tatiana’s choice to be with him in the U.S. When Micah was a little boy he recounts a dream where an alien girl visited him. He found the girl deeply comforting. 

Tatiana loves that Micah is true to himself. She remans sitting next to him at Elizabeth’s Christmas party when he put his napkin in his shirt to eat. No one else did. She thought he was joking, and looked around for others to laugh. But no, this was just Micah being himself. She remarks that he confident that his friends and family will love him just as he is. Shouldn’t everyone be this way?

At this Christmas party, Micah would ask Tatiana if she liked games. Tatiana thought: you don’t even know who are talking to. As an only child who never had to compete, Micah was not ready for Tatiana’s competitiveness. He remembered a Mae Jong game where Tatiana, realizing she wouldn’t win, successfully sabotaged him. Micah remarks of playing games with her: Her full intelligence was on display. 

She remembers when Micah calmly and confidently stood up for her at a movie theater. He was calm but insistent. He stayed on the right side. She was drawn to this integrity. 

Tatiana is still surprised she fell in love with someone who doesn’t speak her first language. For all her strengths, she felt behind her friends in English. Yet she remarks “I found the one whom my soul loves”
