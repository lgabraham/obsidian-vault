Style Guie

1. Clarity and Conciseness: Ensure that your writing is clear and concise. Use simple language and avoid unnecessary jargon or technical terms that may confuse the reader.
2. Organized Structure: Organize your article into sections or subheadings to create a clear and easy-to-follow structure. Use bullet points or numbered lists to highlight important information.
3. Examples and Suggestions: Provide examples and suggestions to help the reader understand the concepts you’re discussing. Use images or visual aids where appropriate to enhance the reader’s understanding.
4. Accessibility: Use language that is easy to understand and accessible to a broad audience. Avoid using overly complex sentences or technical terms that may alienate or confuse the reader.
5. Consistency: Ensure that your writing style and tone are consistent throughout the article. Use a consistent voice and tone to maintain the reader’s engagement.


Questions

1. Can you tell our readers a little about your professional journey & how did you come to your current role/position?

This job is a gift. I never expected to be here. Out of college, I joined a consulting firm. I felt antsy. I began riding a bicycle taxi for work. I started a bicycle taxi company that grew to have ~100 operators. I got an MBA.

Dane is a dear friend from college. We stayed in touch. We would go on long walks — check in on each other’s journeys and challenge each other to grow. Dane started two companies. While pondering his third, he asked me to join him. I said yes. We knew nothing about furniture. Nor did we start there.

I did everything I could to learn about why people cared about the problem we stumbled on — online furniture shopping being so frustrating and chaotic. Everything we learned compounded.

2. Who has influenced you the most in life and why?

My father and grandfather. I have their name. My grandfather immigrated from Lebanon. He started with nothing. He shined shoes and worked in coal mines. With his mind and incredible energy output, he made a life for him and his family.

My father took his own great risks. He started some 13 businesses. Most failed or were eventually wound down. One succeeded. He was always trying new things with incredible creativity bordering on play.

3. What are the biggest challenges you have faced in your life & how did you overcome them?

My father died at 64. I am an only child. It happened as I was graduating from my MBA program, as a new chapter was supposed to be opening. Taking care of my mother and my family delayed my career’s launch. Returning to a sense of solidity and forward momentum was a long journey.
