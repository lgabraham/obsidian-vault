# Mom 70th
Dear Mom,

70 years! And I’ve now born witness to over half of them, albeit in varying states of ability. 

I think we are lucky to have found one another. One might say we were made for one another. More precisely, though, we have each helped make one another into who we are today. Certainly, this is it’s own miracle. 

I, for one, feel lucky for many of the ways you have helped shape me. 

Your mere presence lightened the weight of the Abraham crown on my head. This “crown” has many wonderful benefits, of course. But I am glad for the different perspective you provided — and indeed fought hard for.

Your love has always been delightfully free of expectations, outside of the expectations that I be a good person. This is a true gift to me.

My life can also be marked by a constant stream of well-wrapped gifts, cards, and various other cowbell, er, accoutrements. Even though I chafe against excess stuff, I can only imagine that such a persistent steam of love-stuff has help shaped my sense of confidence and wellbeing. 

The arc of your gift-giving has also changed as you have changed. There is more listening in it. There is less, I think, stuff for the sake of stuff.  More loving love-stuff, that is. From a more loving you.

Indeed, while you have always been loving — as showcased in the things mentioned above — you may have in fact be becoming more loving as you also arrive at a greater sense of ease unto yourself.

I am truly proud of who you are, and also who you are becoming. You are one formidable fast-walking, always-dancing, Flutterby. Here is to more trips abroad, more Burning Mans, more pajama Christmases and homemade gyoza, and many adventures of many kinds with Naya.

I love you to the moon and back.

Your son,
Geoff
