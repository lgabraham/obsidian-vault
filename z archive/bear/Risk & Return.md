# Risk & Return
The only way of getting somewhere is to leave something behind. 

To leave safety is to adventure. 
To adventure is to risk. 
To return home is to return to safety. 
To have + share rewards feels good. 
To fail + learn increases knowledge. 

To venture out and come back with more, is a central psychological experience, and also central to a decentralized economy. 

By definition, success cannot be guaranteed. 
By definition, the greatest success is a function of one’s most central insecurity, and pertain to one’s sense of worth. 

LTA: financial insecurity -> stability
LPA: status insecurity -> status
LGA: emotional insecurity -> stability

The emotional metaphor of “return” and financial meaning of “return” are related. 

Risk and return are the hero’s journey, and are analogous to earliest human development. 

By definition, we all begin “safe” in the womb. We are becoming to venture out, to risk. By definition we begin with the protection of our parents. We wander and return. We reference our parents for safety and to understand, until we can do this for ourselves. Some day, we must separate, else we die. Separation can be painful. Must it be?

The hero is called to risk. 
The hero refuses. 
A mentor appears to coax the hero to risk. 
The hero risks. 
To risk is to give up safety, which is life itself (no safety guaranteed). 
Crossing the threshold is a metaphor of moving from safety or parental protection to an exposed world. 
Sometimes the mentor offers symbols to aid the journey. 
At “the inner most cave”, the symbols fail, the hero must offer only himself. 
The hero loses what he knew, what he was, and in risk (courage) is born again. 
The hero returns. 
He returns as mentor and protector (benefiting community), maybe with his own symbols brought from the other place.
