# Work / Funding Request for Unit 3 Roof
HOA team,

Oscar’s proposal for our roof is $23,800 to replace our roof. Given our experiences with Oscar before, I trust him to be fair.

While Mimosa and I have lived under this roof for years, Oscar’s insistence that the roof is “very dangerous” sticks with me. Oscar would be able to start work on Wednesday of this week, once the skies clear.

I don’t believe the HOA should fund the entire cost of the roof. For one thing, on account of the nature of the repair (replacing the entire “collapsed” structure), Oscar will be able to re-pitch the roof to add a feet or so of additional “head room,” which improves our space.

Yet it also feels unfair to me that the HOA would pay nothing. Our unit has dutifully paid into projects unrelated to our structure (retaining wall, the main house roof, etc.) We have been speaking of this project as an HOA priority for some time.

I do not have in mind a compelling principle for a middle ground. What seems to me to be the “fairest” is to limit the amount of money the HOA pays for the project. Perhaps 50% or $10,000 (the prior roof repair) would be a reasonable “cap” for an HOA contribution.

As historical context, until 2019, my structure was considered “separate” form the main house. Since we “condomeniumized”, we have shared expenses as if we are one structure. I am also open to formally returning to the prior arrangement. 

After some reflection, I will formally ask the HOA in this email for $7,500 towards this project. It is a conservative request that reflects I want to, where possible, protect you from coats rather than view you as a funding source. Yet I still hope that you can materially contribute to my structure’s repairs as I materially contribute to yours. Importantly, I don’t care how or when we would receive this money. It could be paired with the sewer lateral assessment, for example. 

I know this may feel tense. It has elements of zero-sum-ness. Perhaps especially given our history where a neighbor “expected” the HOA to fund a surprise in their own project. I don’t have any expectations. I just hope to arrive at a decision that seems fair to the group.

On the bright side, if repairs go well, the roof may be able to sustain a small roof deck (that I would separately fund), that y’all will be welcome to use anytime. The view is epic.

I am eager for your thoughts. I am happy to discuss this by phone or Zoom. I will be around tomorrow if you’d like to speak in person.

I do hope to start the work Wednesday, so I will appreciate your calls, emails, etc. as you process your thoughts.

Thank you for your attention and care.
Geoff & Mimosa
