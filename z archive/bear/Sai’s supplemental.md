# Sai’s supplemental


I graduated from Stanford Business School in 2018 and am currently cofounder of Spoken.io, a YC-backed startup. I write to express my emphatic support for Sai’s admission to Stanford University.

My 2nd interaction with Sai, less than half my age, involved a difficult business conversation. His proposal for my business had been miss-priced. He had to deliver to us the revised, higher price.

Sai handled the delicate matter with total integrity. This impressed me. I have MBA-minted peers who might not have spoken with equal directness and grace. We awarded Sai and his brother our contract, which had been competitive, even though they were the youngest team by several years.

Sai is currently teaching our company how to navigate its most important marketing channel (TikTok). He makes learning fun — for himself and others.  It makes perfect sense he has built a natural following around this. Today he is materially informing our messaging and marketing strategy.

Sai shows unparalleled energy and determination. He listens well. Yet he drives the conversation forward. He is empathetic, creative, and kind. I know Sai will build deep, learning relationships with his peers and professors. I can’t imagine a better environment for him than Stanford. 


Learning — and specifically learning with others — is what excites him. 





My current company represents one of the most significant bets of my life. We are backed by top funders.  Yet we will only succeed if we can build trust with a significant consumer audience. Sai is leading this effort. 

While it is too early to tell whether this bet will be successful, I trust that if any one can succeed, he can. He has done it before. Regardless of outcome, I plan to be speaking with Sai long after this project is finished.

At first glance, what seems rare about Sai is his energy and drive. Yet through working with Sai, I have come to believe that what sets him apart is an others-focus. One that is rare of TikTokers, let alone my peers. 

What is rare about Sai is his X and Y.
