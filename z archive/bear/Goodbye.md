# Goodbye
Remove deaths

Red riders,

On April 1st, the red fleet rides again. Importantly, on this day, I will not call the red trikes my own. 

Cabrio Taxi, 10 this year, has endowed me with more than I could have ever imagined. This organism was the crucible of my beliefs — about labor, lifestyle, dignity; the company is nothing short of an expression of my inner being. Most important of all, our strange world delivered me the happiest bit of my life, my life partner, Mimosa, who is also soon to be the (spoiler) mother of our child. Our partnership was forged in the project of Cabrio Taxi. 

The last act of this run of happy magic has been surfacing someone who I believe will lead this company as I built it. His name is Dan Kerrigan. It is safe to say that no one I know loves pedicabbing more than Dan. For 2 whole years, he threw a pedicab in his pickup truck and roved the nation in search of off-the-beaten-path “pedicab-able” events. 

Dan recently shared with me a set of notes he had shared with a pedicab owner he used to ride for. This, too, is how my story began. Most of the notes pertained to how to make the company more rider-centric. Riders should be treated better. This philosophy — rider-centrism, riders as the customer and therefore “boss” in the owner-rider relationship has bonded us long before today. 

Some three years ago it became clear to me that my time at Cabrio’s masthead was over. (Mimosa may tell you my attention had drifted long before.) Cabrio had given me all; why should I look a gift horse in the mouth? 

The first person I reached out to was Dan. With several small pedicab operation under his belt, and having ridden with us for many years, he had both the competence to run a strong company and a deep understanding of our culture. He once told me that when he saw Cabrio’s shop, and the riders in it, something clicked for him: “this is always how I envisioned it should be.”

I took this praise as sincere. More than praise, these words signified to me a “values alignment”. We cared about the same thing. We still do. Dan cares deeply about taking up the mantle we have all built. 

Let me be clear: what is special in our little company is you, the rider. Not me, nor Dan. You are the hero of the pedicab story. Wether you are riding seasons to travel in winter, staving off the advance of death via movement, or supporting your lifestyle in (admittedly longer than supposed) transition, you are the literal and metaphorical power in the trikes. 

I always felt I was merely building the platform or container for the naturally kind, quirky group of folks who would find their way to pedicab for a living. Perhaps nudging us in a good direction, occasionally, but mostly getting out of y’alls way. I know Dan believes the same. For the foreseeable future, he intends to change little. I will let him follow up with details. 

Thank you for trusting me and Mimosa, insofar as you have, for the last decade. We do plan to be in your lives for many decades to come.

I am excited for the renewal summer brings. I am excited for y’all to rediscover your strength, your friendships, and one of the best lifestyle activities San Francisco knows. I invite you to share with Dan what is important to you about Cabrio Taxi, so that he may listen. I invite you, also, to trust his direction in the coming months. 

I will continue to visit the shop — one might joke, as frequently as I always did. I will be eager to see your faces. 

Colin will be tuning the bikes, so they will be as ready as they ever were. 

Encore. 
G
