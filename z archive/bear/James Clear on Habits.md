# James Clear on Habits
Much of our behavior informed by habits
Many of our outcomes are lagging indicators of our behavior

Our modern environment has longer term reward cycles
Our brain was built in an environment with shorter term reward cycles

The cardinal rule of behavior change: behaviors that get immediately rewarded get repeated the behaviors that get immediately punished get avoided

Speed and intensity of feedback matters

Will power is not a good long term strategy. 

Goals do not differentiate. Presumably all racers have the goal to win. What differentiates then?

Your system, ie your collection of habits, I must align to your goals. 

Your current system is (definitionally) geared for your current results. 

We badly want different results. The results are not the thing that need to change. Change the habits. The outcomes will fix themselves. 

Goals are for people that want to win one time. Systems are for people who want to win again and again. 

You don’t rise to the level of your expectations, you fall to the level of your training. 

You don’t rise to the level of your goals, you fall to the level of your systems. 

Make changes that are small or easy to do. Layer them on top of one another in systems. Collectively, you can get powerful results. 

Why do habits really matter? They get us external things. 

The deeper reason is that they are signals internally to ourselves about who we are. They are like the story we are telling ourselves. 

Every time you perform a habit, you embody a particular identity. 

If you keep performing a behavior enough, you cross a threshold into believing I am someone who X. 

Habits provide evidence (proof) of the story you are telling yourself. 

Often, we start with an outcome, and back into a process. If I do those things, I’ll be the person I want to be?

What if we flipped this and asked: who is the person I want to be? One step further, we can ask a person who has that identity, what kind of habits do they have? Then we use the identity to inform the habits. 

What would a X person do? (Healthy, empowered, confident, body-positive)

If you don’t have a shift in internal story yet, it’s hard for the habits to follow suit. 

What’s you get to a place of identity shift, you are in a much more powerful place from the standpoint of behavior change.

You’re not trying to change any more. You are just acting in alignment with the person you see yourself to be. 

Changing through epiphany may not be a reliable way of changing behavior, or a way that you can plan around or rely on. 

Every action is a vote for the type of person you wish to become. 

Beliefs without action are delusion. 

You have to genuinely believe the story for the behavior change to work. You can only believe the story if you see evidence. 

How do you genuinely believe if you’re not yet where you want to be? You have to start by changing little habits. 

Measure of whether something is a habit or not is: does it bother you to not do it?

Until you see external validation, you must in love with the system to endure. 

Massive environment changes are a “hack” for a behavior change. 

The more that we take pride in our behavior or habits, the more they tend to stick. 

What parts of your story do you take pride in?

The social environment has large implications. 

Join groups where your desired behavior is the normal behavior. 

Human behavior is mostly predictive. 

The dopamine surge comes from the anticipation of the reward, not the reward. 

Dopamine is a teaching molecule — a learning molecule. It helps you mark experiences, and spikes when you encounter a similar cue. 

Drugs hack the system. The process of learning is broken. Addicts know the behavior affects their lives but still can’t get themselves to stop doing it. 

In a sense, habits are solutions to recurring problems you face. 

Cue, craving (meaning), response, reward 

Make habits obvious, attractive, easy, satisfying. 

The most addictive behaviors are variably rewarding. 

Environment is like a form of gravity. 

**Never miss twice.**

You might **be** the victim. But you never have to **play** the victim — have it become part of your identity, believe it’s inevitable, etc. 

Know your ABZs. A is first step. B is 2nd. Z is the goal. I need to know that B is directionally correct. If it is, I can just run ABZ until I get to my goal. 

Work backwards from magic. What would a magical outcome be?

Most people become their own bottle neck long before reality prevents them from doing it.

I want to be clear about where I am going but flexible about how I get there.
