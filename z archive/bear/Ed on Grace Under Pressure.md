# Ed on Grace Under Pressure
“They’re inviting me onto their boat” is beautiful. It reminds me of the respect I implicitly owe to any being inviting me into their home, yet the boat connotes journey.

You have to be humble as a coach. I play a useful role, but I also play a small role.

Layoffs can lack grace and not afford people dignity. 

“Soft culture.” Don’t want to be a daycare. The challenge for leaders is to create a high accountability high empathy culture. 

Conflict avoidance — the “disease of nice” Amy edmunsen at HBS. Psychological safety is not about being nice. It’s about creating an environment where people feel free to speak up without reprisal. If people are “nice” and afraid to be direct, that’s psychologically unsafe. Niceness can become a constraint that prevents us from having high accountability cultures. Let’s not substitute niceness for empathy. 

Tyranny of feelings — privileging the subjective experience. “You are making me feel this way; you need to stop.” That’s the logic of a child. 

The contagiousness of a leader’s emotions. Everyone is looking to the leader to understand “what is the leader feeling”. If the leader is not regulated and communicative, they will be less effective. 

Emotional suppression: “pretending I’m not feeling what I’m feeling, and hoping the feeling goes away.”

Being authentic is not saying whatever comes to your mind. Might feel good but may not help you accomplish your goals. 

The “created self” and the “discovered self”. The authentic self we can discover and create. (Like Girard, no inherent desire — only the desire we model by observing others)

Authenticity vs comfort; if we have to create ourselves, we have to encounter new territory. If we define discomfort as inauthentic, we’re blocking our own growth. 

Leadership is an act. (One demanded of our most tribal and ancestral selves, per Girard.) Leadership as a performing art. I’m going to adopt the persona that is going to allow me to accomplish my goals. Also the symbolic aspect of leadership. Not just decision making. Leadership is embodying a persona the organization (not just companies but all human groups) requires. They serve a unique function. **What is this function?**

Learning is more important than teaching. Leadership is not exhortation. Leaders can also have in their motivational repertoire that they can create the conditions where people unlock (or tap into) their own internal motivations. Not just about creating external forces that drive. (This is a form, but insufficient.) All of the extrinsic factors leaders rely on are finite and limited. If instead, you tap into people’s inner motivations, intrinsically inspires. 

Grief can be a path to grace if that is what you choose. We may experience loss that we have been reluctant to grieve. Loss has legitimacy no matter how small.
