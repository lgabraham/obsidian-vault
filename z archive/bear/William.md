**The William Schulz House, 1908**

While Behrend was planning the William Schulz residence, he designed a barn for Adolph Sutro’s property at the City’s edge. Sutro’s John McLaren house with thirty-two horses cost $4,000. In comparison, William Schulz’s house on Grove Street cost only $3,000. But then its backyard stable sheltered only one horse. Now more than a century later, Adolph Sutro’s barn and other structures are gone, but William Schulz’s property survives. It consists of four tenancy common units: three at 957 and one at 955—the old house numbered 955 Grove, its listing price in 2007 was $939,000.

*Article written June 2007*
