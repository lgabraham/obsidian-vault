# Marie

Marie,
This weekend, at a wedding for a GSB friend, I met a zen practitioner, with whom I had a wonderful conversation. He remarked how impressed he was GSB students seemed interested in deep questions like he was.  



I told him the story of how Stanford became so special (your story). I told him of when I first encountered the question “What matters most to you and why?” helping my friend write his application, and how this inspired me to apply. How I then ultimately met you, learned why you had written the question, then come to understand first hand how the question would shape our class. 



What a gift you gave to that institution, to so many in it without their fully knowing. What a gift, certainly, you gave to me. 



The social fabric that I nurture from my time at the GSB continues to be one of the most meaningful things in my life (5 wedding just this year!). Mimosa and Naya are deeply integrated into this lovely web. Moreover, I deeply resonate with the “why” of my friends, even as they continue to refine and newly articulate it. 



Thank you for being the change you wished to see in the world, and for setting the tone for the culture that today I count an important part of my identity. These relationships are the greatest wealth I could have imagined. We are so much richer for your courage to prompt us to more deeply understand ourselves from even before we step foot on campus.
