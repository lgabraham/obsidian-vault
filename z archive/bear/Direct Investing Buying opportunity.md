# Direct Investing Buying opportunity

Given (1) my desire for a simple set of wealth, (2) my projected income, costs, and net transfers, and (3) my understanding of economic trends, 2025 or 2026 could offer a buying opportunity to secure US equities at a good cost basis. 

I have historically “sat on the sidelines” not simply for emotional reasons (uncertainty) but also because my cash needs were not clear. (Did I want to buy a house?)

The setup: stocks are historically pricey, interest rates are high (not fully absorbed), unemployment is turning (not sure what this means, fully), leadership is historically chaotic (tariffs), and the fed chair will surely be replaced in 2026 for a dove. 

If a recession occurs, a buying opportunity in broad based US equities would be: S&P $4,500- $5,000. 

One thing that has been obvious in writing this is time in the market conquers all else. 

If I am mostly funded through the end of 2026, and I am in touch with my security, my excess cash (save ~$150,000K?) would do better in direct investing in Wealthfront. 

The best time to begin compounding is yesterday. The next best time is today. 

In my 2027 start financial imagining (roughly $2m cash, $1m home equity), the strongest position would be to have some income cover costs, and invest $1.8m in the market over the long term. 

The point of this note and thinking is that I may have the opportunity to build a cost basis I can feel confident about soon.
