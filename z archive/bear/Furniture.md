Furniture is a $250B market in the U.S. that grows around 3% each year. Online sales amount to $50B that grow about 20% each year. Of this, Wayfair accounts for nearly $15B and grows at 60% per year. 

This speaks to 2 trends. A an increasing share of online furniture purchasing moving online, and the gains accrue interest to internet-first companies. (Wayfair owns no inventory.)

Yet if you speak to anyone who has done it recently, buying furniture online is an enormously frustrating experience. 

Even offline, furniture margins range 200%-400%.  The primary strategy of online furniture brands — which often, like Wayfair, own no inventory —  is to merchandise and curate items as a means to extract a premium. 

There are only several handfuls of important furniture distributors. Many of their items are sold online across the largest retailers — Amazon, Walmart, etc. Many of them also are merchandised and curated to generate margin. 

The result is that you may find the exact same item listed in multiple places at wildly different prices. An otherwise trusted furniture brands that will remain unnamed may price an item you can find on Amazon for $900 at $7,000. 50% off for you, of course. 

Even on Wayfair — which began integrated many websites and still aggregates many resellers who merchandise and curate — you may find a “duplicate” item at different prices. 

The problem is exacerbated by the incentive of the margins of the home category. Almost any trusted brand has great incentive to extend their brand into Home by hiring someone to curate a collection of home goods. 

The ultimate result of all of this is lost trust for the consumer who does not have a map for this territory.

Google may in fact know many of the prices across retailers. But highlighting them would jeapordize their ad revenue. 

We are making the map. Without mixed incentives. We merely collect where you can buy furniture items at what prices — even if the item was renamed ie “merchandised”. 

The first result is a Google Flights for furniture where that delivers real-time, actionable information, including pricing and inventory. Furniture is nearly unsearchable on Google. It will be searchable on Spoken. 

The ultimate result will be trust with end users.
