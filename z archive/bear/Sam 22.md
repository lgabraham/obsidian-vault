I would only consider a coaching relationship if I were able to participate in the value our relationship unlocked. I would need to take “downside” risk too. The reason is primarily motivational. 

I also hope to explore new (to me) coaching models besides hourly rates. We may have enough trust to navigate both. 

If there are outcomes where I “eat well” there should be outcomes where I don’t eat at all. 

I have not thought of this before. I have begun workshopping ideas. 

Here is how I imagine we might work:

We decide on an hourly rate. I believe Jeff Levere started his practice at $200/hr. We meet each week. 

We hold the funds in “escrow” — an account you see and I manage.

If at any point you decide our time is not worthwhile, I return the entire sum of money to you without question or regret.

If you successfully achieve an exit, and you cannot honestly say I did not make a meaningful difference in your outcome, I earn this sum. 

If you successfully exit, and you believe I meaningfully impacted your outcome, you would compensate me as if the entire sum of money were converted into common equity at the $140M purchase price. You will have a better understanding of what the appropriate formula should be and I invite your input. I care more about the principle than the math. 

You would have to “cover” the difference from the winnings of your own outcome. 

For simple math, if we meet an hour each week for 50 weeks, this is $5,000 each year or $35,000 over 7 years. If you decided in 2023 that we were not working, I would return every dollar you’ve paid me and thank you for the lesson and your time.  would pay me as if the $35,000 were common equity at purchase. 

I would, with your consent, hold the funds in an S&P 500 index. You would receive the appreciation in the “fail” case. You would only cover the difference between S&P 500 and your outcome in the “success” case. 

Most of this would be “notional” and not contractual. I don’t wish to write down terms for our mutual understanding, look each other in the eye, shake hands and otherwise forget about terms. 

Every other consideration I would decide with a heuristic of simplicity out of deference to our relationship. You would receive red lines within 24hrs. 

In my ideal world, I would ask you to meet with another coach for some number of sessions to determine whether or not I was any good relative said other coach. They may come with resources or network or any number of things I can’t imagine that would warrant your not “risking” paying me upside. 

I may be better than an average coach but not **this much** better. 

My intent, for now, is to only take you as a client.
