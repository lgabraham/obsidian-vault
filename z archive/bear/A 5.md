# A

was to understand the relationship between us and God in terms of the relationship between bride and groom, wife and husband

A fun anthropological building of the Abrahamic family. It synthesizes a number of big historical ideas — monogamy as a force of social equity, experience of God as arising from a loving relationship with one’s spouse, and the family as a powerful social platform. For me the article highlights how enduring of a kind a spiritual practice family formation is. One of my favorite lines: “Science takes things apart to see how they work. Religion puts things together to see what they mean.” My own family is not explicitly Abrahamic, but I do now better appreciate our Abrahamic antecedents.
