# 🧭 Geoff’s Cybernetic Dashboard
### 
This is not a static to-do list. It’s a map of your **active problems** — in the Popper–Deutsch sense — paired with the kind of **feedback** you might monitor and the **mechanisms** you could use to steer.

⸻

### 1. ⚖️ Problem: Sustaining Meaning in a Life of Possibility

**Goal:** Stay engaged, energized, and proud in a life where necessity is no longer the driver.

**Signals of Alignment:**
* You feel a sense of internal pull toward your work.
* You finish days with a feeling of “earned fatigue.”
* You wake up with at least one thing you’re eager to move forward.

**Possible Feedback Loops:**
* Weekly check-in: “What felt meaningful this week? What didn’t?”
* Track how often you share your work or ideas with people you respect.

**Steering Levers:**
* Create “theory of change” docs for your projects — clarify *why* this matters now.
* Seek out conversations with people further ahead who’ve maintained their fire.

⸻

### 2. 🧠 Problem: Deep Work vs. Broad Engagement

**Goal:** Find the right mix of exploratory curiosity and focused creation.

**Signals of Alignment:**
* You feel satisfied after deep work sessions — not scattered.
* You can name the 1–2 things you’re “building” over time.

**Possible Feedback Loops:**
* Each month, note: *What did I go deeper in? What do I want to go deeper in next?*
* Time-tracking or journaling: Are my best hours aligned with my most valued focus?

**Steering Levers:**
* Commit to one “keystone” creative or strategic output per quarter (writing, product, etc.)
* Alternate between “exploration weeks” and “consolidation weeks”

⸻

### 3. ❤️ Problem: Intimacy vs. Independence

**Goal:** Grow into a fuller self while staying emotionally connected and interdependent.

**Signals of Alignment:**
* You feel proud *of yourself* even when no one else is watching.
* You have moments of both deep connection and deep self-direction each week.

**Possible Feedback Loops:**
* Regular self-reflection: *Where did I choose me? Where did I choose us? Was it balanced?*
* Feedback from Mimosa or others on whether you’re bringing your full self into connection

**Steering Levers:**
* Solo retreats or time-blocks for high-agency self-development
* Shared rituals that affirm both independence and togetherness (e.g. “I’m building this” time + “We’re building this” time)

⸻

### 4. 🚀 Problem: Right-Sized Ambition

**Goal:** Aim big enough to stretch and satisfy, but not so big that it erodes rhythm or presence.

**Signals of Alignment:**
* You feel nervous in a good way about what you’re pursuing.
* You’re not overwhelmed, but you are “on the edge” of your current capabilities.

**Possible Feedback Loops:**
* Quarterly “ambition audit”: Is this challenge worthy of me *today*?
* Emotional state tracking: Burnout = too much. Boredom = too little.

**Steering Levers:**
* Re-scope projects based on energy, not ego.
* Talk with mentors who’ve calibrated ambition well.

⸻

### 5. 🧘‍♂️ Problem: Self-Knowledge in a Noisy World

**Goal:** Cultivate deep, embodied self-awareness and agency amid distraction, optimization, and performance culture.

**Signals of Alignment:**
* You feel calm in your center, even while engaging the world.
* You experience pleasure or flow in simple actions — walking, eating, parenting, breathing.

**Possible Feedback Loops:**
* Daily or weekly mindfulness/heart-rate/energy journaling
* Re-read a weekly reflection from a past self: *Am I more myself now?*

**Steering Levers:**
* Make space for boredom and slow time
* Experiment with “low-stimulation days” as reset points
* Keep reconnecting with the “why” behind every system or metric

⸻

### 🧩 Meta-Loop: Problem Evolution

A final layer of your dashboard is meta-reflective:
* Every 3–6 months, review your problems: *Are these still the right ones? Have new ones emerged?*
* As Deutsch would say: the best problems aren’t solved — they are **transformed** into better problems.
