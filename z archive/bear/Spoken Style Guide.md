# Spoken Style Guide
* **Modern (including Minimalist)**: This is a great umbrella term for styles characterized by simplicity and a lack of clutter, focusing on functionality and clean lines.
* **Traditional (including French Country and Transitional)**: This is a good choice for styles that emphasize classic, intricate designs, often with rich colors and textures.
* **Rustic (including Farmhouse)**: Perfect for styles that are more natural, rough, and aged, often using raw wood and stone.
* **Industrial**: This style is distinct enough to stand alone, known for its use of exposed steel, distressed wooden elements, and exposed brick.
* **Scandinavian (including Japandi)**: This is a fitting category for designs that are minimalist yet warm, often featuring light colors, natural materials, and clean lines.
* **Coastal**: This style is unique in its use of light, breezy color palettes inspired by the beach and ocean, making it distinct from the others.
* **Bohemian (including Shabby Chic)**: Bohemian style is diverse and eclectic, making it a good umbrella for Shabby Chic, which shares a similar love for vintage and handmade items.
* **Art Deco**: This style is iconic and distinct with its bold geometric patterns and luxurious materials, deserving its own category.
* **Luxurious (including Hollywood Regency)**: This category can encompass styles that are opulent and elegant, where Hollywood Regency fits well with its glamorous and dramatic flair.
* **Mid-Century Modern**: This style is unique in its retro yet timeless appeal, characterized by clean lines, gentle organic curves, and a love for different materials and forms.
