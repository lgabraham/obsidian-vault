# The Obstacle is the Way
Gents, Ben recently read Meditations, it's one of my faves. Highly recommend to all. It reminded me of a book I'd read that pulled together stoic philosophy into practical little nuggets, each with a tight quote and some elaboration. I typed out the sections and related quotes. Hopefully of some use as you go blazing the path

~~---------------~~
The Obstacle is the Way

The obstacle in the path becomes the path. Never forget, within every obstacle is an opportunity to improve our condition

The Way Through Them
(Marcus Aurelius)
Objective judgment, now at this very moment
Unselfish action, now at this very moment
Willing acceptance - now at this very moment - of all external events
That's all you need

Part I - Perception

Recognize Your Power
(Marcus Aurelius)
Choose not to be harmed - and you won't feel harmed
Don't feel harmed - and you haven't been

Steady Your Nerves
(Theodore Roosevelt)
What such a man needs is not courage but nerve control, cool headedness.
This he can get only by practice

Control Your Emotions
(Publius Syrus)
Would you have a great empire? Rule over yourself.

Practice Objectivity
(Epictetus)
Don't let the force of an impression when it first hit you knock you off your feet; just say to it: Hold on a moment; let me see who you are and what you represent. Let me put you to the test

Alter Your Perspective
(Viktor Frankl)
Man does not simply exist but always decides what his existence will be, what he will become the next moment.
By the same token, every human being has the freedom to change at any instant

Is It Up to You?
(Epictetus)
In life our first job is this, to divide and distinguish things into two categories:  externals I cannot controls, but the choices I make with regard to them I do control. Where will I find good and bad? In me, in my choices

Live in the Present Moment
(Chuck Palahniuk)
The trick to forgetting the big picture is to look at everything close up
(just do it, stop waiting, there's always a better time to be doing something)

Think Differently
(F Scott Fitzgerald)
Genius is the ability to put into effect what is in your mind.
There's no other definition of it

Finding the Opportunity
(Seneca)
A good person dyes events with his own color...and turns whatever happens to his own benefits

Prepare to Act
(Shakespeare)
Then imitate the action of the tiger; stiffen the sinews, summon up the blood

Get Moving
(Theodore Roosevelt)

Iterate

(Heraclitus)

Facing, in fact, every unfavorable condition rather than accept the risk of stalemate invited by direct approach.
