# Cabrio Comms Oct 2020
Hello all,

I hope that y’all are thriving in your own way. I have heard stories from some of you that seem to indicate we are a resilient, adaptable bunch. Not that there isn’t suffering, too. 

SF Pedicabs and Golden Gate Pedicabs have put a few trikes on the road. I want to update you on where Cabrio stands. 

On account of our loss history, our LLC is “uninsurable” through the normal channels. We still have one option for insurance. Yet in addition to a higher insurance premium — which, until Covid, we simply stomached — we also have an insurance minimum, which makes it infeasible to operate a small number of trikes without losing money. 

We did receive a small PPP loan that enabled us to cover operational costs even while not generating income. The Port has also been generous on rent deferral. I am working on a solution to the above insurance hurdle so that we may fully open in 2021. 

I personally feel the pressure of the Federal stimulus package running dry. Our loan is nearly used up. Moreover, it is painful to me to know your aid is drying up and so your interest in riding is on the rise. Yet we still are not open. 

In limited circumstances (“I will be a new dad”), I devised a workaround to put someone on a trike. Unfortunately, this solution does not scale without putting me at great personal risk. 

In the meantime, I know some of you have taken out a trike with another company. I fully support each and every one of you to do what you need to do to provide for yourselves. 

I look to when I can communicate clearly to y’all how the red trikes will return to the road. They surely will. Thank you for your interest and loyalty as we work through this. It means much to us. 

Godspeed,
Geoff
