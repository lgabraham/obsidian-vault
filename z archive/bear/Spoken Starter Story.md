# Spoken Starter Story
**Hello! Who are you and what business did you start?**
I'm Geoff Abraham. My dear friend of 18 years, Dane Hurtubise and I launched Spoken—a digital platform that identifies the best deals for furniture across top online retailers. We’re tackling the confusing issue of identical products with varying brands and prices. Our goal? Make online furniture shopping a breeze. Since our launch, our user base has skyrocketed, reaching hundreds of thousands monthly.

**What's your backstory and how did you come up with the idea?**
Both entrepreneurs, Dane sold software company Parklet, and I sold Cabrio Taxi, an outdoor advertising venture, in 2021. Our eureka moment? Dane found the same coffee table at Urban Outfitters and Home Depot with a significant price difference. Investigating, we noticed products often got rebranded and sold at varying prices, confusing customers. Seeing this gap, we envisioned Spoken.

**Take us through the process of building the first version of Spoken.**
Dane single handedly coded our inaugural site, which showcased a curated list of 50 products. The buzz on Reddit following its launch was more than validation—it was a sign. Seizing this momentum, we quickly expanded our inventory to 20,000 products. The highlight? Spoken featuring prominently on Reddit's homepage.

**Describe the process of launching the business.**
Our initial stride into the market was marked by a lean version of Spoken, which immediately caught Reddit's attention. This early affirmation fueled our drive, prompting rapid expansion. Our growth wasn't just in product numbers; a coveted spot on Reddit's homepage bolstered our credibility. While we self-funded the initial phases, joining Y Combinator marked a significant milestone.

**Since launch, what has propelled Spoken's growth?**
Our focus on organic growth, aided by moments on platforms like Reddit and TikTok, has been pivotal. A viral TikTok video by Preston Konrad serves as a prime example of our outreach. Our genuine approach, paired with insights into Gen Z and millennial preferences, has continually worked to our advantage.

**How are you doing today and what does the future look like?**
Spoken is on the brink of expansion. We see a future where online shopping pivots from store-focus to shopper-focus, empowering individuals like never before. We like to believe we're not just selling furniture; we're reinventing online commerce.

**Through starting the business, have you learned anything particularly helpful or advantageous?**
Community is everything. Platforms like Reddit offered both exposure and invaluable feedback. Staying flexible and receptive to user insights is vital in the tech world.

**What platform/tools do you use for your business?**
OpenAI's GPT drives our idea generation and content. Our team operates on Discord and Notion, supplemented by tools like PostHog, SEMRush, Resend, Algolia, and Ghost.

**What have been the most influential books, podcasts, or other resources?**
Paul Graham's essays have deeply influenced me. "How to Work Hard" and "A Project of One's Own" top my list, offering wisdom on meaningful work and the value of personal projects.

**Advice for other entrepreneurs who want to get started or are just starting out?**
Stay genuine. Embrace feedback. Treat setbacks as growth opportunities. And always chase passion.
