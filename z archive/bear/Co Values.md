# Co Values

Work to live. 1. This work is meaningful to us; it animates our life, defines us; we also protect our separate, meaningful lives  from our work. 

Play. When possible, we play. When we play, we grow. Play allows us to experiment and access our selves, creativity and risk-taking. 

Note: What feels like play to us may look like work to others. 

One team. When we are together on the field (at work), we are one team. We make space for all experiences in the privacy of our (virtual) office. Externally, we are, while diverse in composition, a monolith.  

Momentum. We are dying. Sacrifice perfect for forward and good.

My grandfather began his professional career as — an immigrant in a foreign land — by building a toolbox; he carried the box onto a construction site as day labor. Others made fun of him for how poorly the box was made. He would go on to build a successful company as a builder, send 3 kids to college, live an epic love story, and generally actualize the American Dream.

His journey has always inspired me. Central to its meaning to me is the dignity of labor and the capacity of good work to transform a life. I too began my professional life as a laborer, pedaling a bicycle taxi. Then the dignity of labor — and enabling pride in work — became a primary motivation of my first company, a bicycle taxi company in San Francisco, which I ran for 10 years that still operates today. 

I still have my grandfather’s toolbox in my office; it still inspires me. The central learning of my first, low-tech company: to flourish in a modern world, one must employ modern tools. I want to help build part of a toolset that can enable individual workers to flourish in their work.
