# Explorer	
Team, Andrew and I sat down to divide and conquer the $5,500 AngelList Management Company Offering. If y’all remember, we believed we would be able to take on the list for less than $55K for 10 years.

After reviewing the items in depth, we believe we should instead pay this fee, and spend our incremental hours trying to arrive properly at $4M.

As a data point, Spoken’s annual accounting service is $3,500. Quarterly service would be $6,800. The AngelList offering not only includes quarterly filings, it includes a slew of other things, some of which Andrew and I would be doing for the first time (ERA filings, K-1s), which would increase chance of error.

We do want to be lean. And this would increase the base cost of the fund by 20%. Yet we likely couldn’t beat this price by much. And we do want to — to your point with the EA, Rish — prioritize the right things. The incremental hour spent getting in front of good founders seems like a better use of our time.

To this end, Andrew and I are sitting in a hotel lobby drafting our emails to classmates per Marcin’s push.
