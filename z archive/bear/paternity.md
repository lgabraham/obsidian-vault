# paternity

My friend,

Before I leave for the 2nd part of my paternity leave, after the Allstate call, I’ll etch my present mind state into Discord.

We’re in an existential moment. I feel the  weight of it. For some time, I was angry that we arrived at a place where I felt forced to choose between my family and our company — two things I love. I had designed this paternity leave as best I could around the needs of our work. I was angry you hadn’t acted urgently sooner, and angry at myself for hoping you would, without saying so. 

At present, though, I am surrendering to where we are. I don’t mean giving up. I mean trusting that we will navigate what comes. I trust that my personal priority of a stretch of deep presence with my family — the disqualifying boundary condition I named when joining our cofoundership — can coexist with the consequences of our decisions so far, and where that puts us.

I’d guess the next 15 days will feel hard when you’d prefer I were present, or at least, that you weren’t alone. I appreciate you. I believe in you. I’ll aim to be with you when it most matters while honoring presence with my family. Take care of yourself. It seems like you are already doing a great job of this. 

Building this with you has been a rich project of my life. If the chapter ends, may it do so sanely enough that we will laugh wholeheartedly about the chaos. If it continues, I’ll make sure for myself it is from a place of renewed energy and conviction. I trust you’ll do the same.
