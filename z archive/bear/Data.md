# Data

When looking to analyze trends related to moving, home buying, renting, and furniture buying in the US, you can consider several valuable datasets available from a variety of sources. Here are some key datasets and where to find them:

1. **U.S. Census Bureau Data**:
   - **American Community Survey (ACS)**: Offers detailed annual data on housing characteristics, including data on movers, renters, and homeowners. You can explore variables like duration of residence, rental rates, and more.
   - **Decennial Census**: Provides comprehensive data every ten years, useful for long-term trend analysis in housing.
   - Access these datasets via [The U.S. Census Bureau’s website](https://www.census.gov).

2. **Zillow Research Data**:
   - Offers a range of datasets on home values, rents, and trends in real estate markets. Zillow’s data can be particularly useful for analyzing housing market trends and real estate dynamics.
   - Available at [Zillow Research](https://www.zillow.com/research/data).

3. **U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD)**:
   - **HUD USER Datasets**: Provides data on housing market conditions, housing affordability, and HUD’s programs. Useful for analyzing public policy impacts on housing.
   - Visit [HUD USER](https://www.huduser.gov/portal/datasets.html) for data access.

4. **Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS)**:
   - **Consumer Expenditure Survey**: Offers data on the buying habits of Americans, including spending on furniture and household appliances, which can be useful for furniture buying trends.
   - Find this data on the [BLS website](https://www.bls.gov/cex/).

5. **Federal Housing Finance Agency (FHFA)**:
   - **House Price Index (HPI)**: Tracks changes in the value of residential real estate both nationally and regionally. This can help understand trends in home buying and selling prices.
   - Access it via [FHFA’s website](https://www.fhfa.gov/DataTools/Downloads/Pages/House-Price-Index.aspx).

6. **National Association of Realtors (NAR)**:
   - Provides various reports and datasets on home buying, selling, and real estate market trends. Their data includes regional and national statistics, which can be useful for a nuanced analysis.
   - Check out [NAR’s research and statistics](https://www.nar.realtor/research-and-statistics).

These datasets will help you gather comprehensive insights into the dynamics of moving, home buying, renting, and furniture buying in the US. You can download the data directly from the provided websites, often requiring registration or a simple data request form.
