# Hearing


Dear Mr. O’Riordan,

In summary, I am making good faith efforts to modify and legalize my home given my understanding of the associated processes. 

I removed the structure (roof deck and stairs), as soon as I was told in February by SF Planning and Zoning it would not be approved.  I am revising a permit for the elevated pitch of the roof window replacement.

This has all been *enormously* painful to me and my family. I do not seek your sympathy, per se. I merely ask you allow me to proceed with the only path I see to legalization.

Thank you.
Geoff

I’ve also included more color below, summarizing events and referencing documents for the record of the hearing.

In February of 2021, I contracted Oscar Contreras of SF Quality Roofing for emergency roof repair. The roof was sagged 6 inches over about 12 feet, and a large rubber tree drops consistent foliage on the roof. 

I regret many things about this relationship. One regret is believing what Oscar told me required permitting. He suggested altering the pitch of the roof, which would elevate the roof ~2 feet in the rear, would be covered by the reroofing permit. (This would allow me to not duck when entering my bedroom.) Because my unit, a small former horse stable, is nonconforming, this elevation happened on the property line.

My chief regret was allowing Oscar to convince me that any required permitting and construction could happen in parallel. I understand now that it was my responsibility, not his, to verify this information. 

From the first notice of violation, I have responded in a timely and direct manner. I invited Duffy to the site days after we received the first complaint and associated notice of violation. 

Duffy told me then he could not remove the building violation since the work had violated a planning code violation as well.  Shortly thereafter, our reroofing work — including the altered pitch — was inspected and approved (permit 2021-0318-6793).

In September of 2021 I had my first conversation with Dario in Planning and Zoning. In October I began working on an application for variance that would allow me to then properly permit the now extant structure. 

I submitted the variance in December (2021-012822PRJ). My wife returned to the permit office in March and was told to notify the office once we were ready to apply for a permit.

I met with Dario and Michelle in Planning and Zoning over February and March. We scheduled a community meeting for June 22, 2002. In May, Dario told me that he thought the chances of approval were low. I proposed in a May 17th email removing the deck structure, and restricting the variance application to the altered roof pitch. He did not respond.

I was surprised in January to receive the Notice of Hearing. I did not understand the variance application process and notice of violation process were separate. I had believed my case to be stuck in limbo. 

I take responsibility for this delay, as well. My first child was born in August of 2021. I started a business in 2022 and have been busy trying to provide for my family and be an involved father. In the meantime, the stairs and roof deck were roped off and entirely unused. 

I quickly responded to Gilbert and notified him of the variance application. He convened with Dario, who then responded to my May17th email stating the deck and stairs should be removed. I removed the deck and stairs the following week, notifying Gilbert.

My wife again returned to the permitting office this week to pull a permit for the elevated roof pitch and replacement of windows. I hoped to have this permit in process ahead of our hearing.  She was unable to do so on account of our drawings not having a wet signature of a certified structural engineer. We are now in conversations with a such a professional (Jefferson Chen) to produce drawings to pull this permit as soon as we’re able.

This has all been *enormously* painful to me and my family. My mistake of following Oscar’s lead will cost me tens of thousands of dollars, and much lost sleep. I do not seek your sympathy per se. I merely ask for a clear path to legalization. Thank you for your attention.
