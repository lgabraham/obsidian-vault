# Dear Dad
Dad, it is surreal to now know a day truly without you. You enabled all that I am. You were foundation, safety net, arch support, model, guide, inspiration, visionary, and life-long best friend. You forced joy and pride and integrity into this world, and you grew frustrated when you witnessed others settle for less. You imagined and executed project after project, serially and without pause. You showed us all that life can be — why wouldn’t it be? — a grand adventure. Even with your gregarious temperament that could border on brash, you surprised me, again and again, at your ability to be wise, and to be big-hearted. 

You may well have left at your happiest. You and Mom were so beautifully priming your next chapter of life. I find some comfort in thinking you left quickly, and at a peak. But I admit I am struggling to sat final goodbye to the 20 (or 30) years I had planned for us to eagerly and jointly live. There are too many summers to fish, too many milestones to celebrate, too many projects to complete together. You were far too young, and you had set up too happy of a future to not live it out completely. 

I do now feel a little lost, or perhaps more specifically something like “without father”.  I know I am blessed to have lived the 32 years I did with you. But I was not nearly finished downloading your wisdom, seeking your advice, or merely sharing in your company. You always knew how to turn what first looked like a problem into an opportunity. You knew how to touch something, and make it gold. You knew, so deeply, how to build a sense of belonging and an aesthetic of familial warmth. Yours shoes are a size and kind that can not be filled. 

I am not anymore whole. I will heal. So will we all — all of the souls that you so affected with your outsized personality and that beautiful, romantic lens through which you viewed the world. My heart breaks for your wife, who was so relishing the new life you were courageously constructing, as y’all were only now discovering new corners to love in one another. It breaks, too, for your friends and family who will miss your laughter and presence that animated their days with new light. 

We will be sure to throw you a big party — one that celebrates a life as big as yours. In the meanwhile, on your elsewhere journey, know that my love for you burns bright as ever, and I miss you dearly. 

Per a friend: Li khallaf ma mat. 

Your son. 

—-

Mayor Larry Abraham died on May 5th. He went quickly, due to heart complications; he had been happy in the days and hours leading to his death. The family is seeking space and privacy in their grief. A celebration of Larry’s life will be held later this year. More soon. Love to all.
