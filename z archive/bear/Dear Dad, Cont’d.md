# Dear Dad, Cont’d
I hear him now, in the morning birds. His pride is in their song. His charisma and color, too. He always did rise early, and occupy the peace of the morning in contentment and reflection. Even in the face of an overwhelming eagerness to play and build the length of the day, he could sit quietly and relish his small, but happy — and proud — dominion. I hadn’t appreciated this before — his peace. But now I hear it in the way the birdsong hangs, “tranquilo,” in the quiet morning air. 

I found this photo, some hours after posting this, from Father’s Day 2017 — at Aunt Karen’s park naming ceremony at UNM. (We don’t look great.) I had not ever looked closely at the plaque; nor did I have a clue what it said. My Dad always did enjoy little tricks and grand reveals. Father’s Day 2018 is Stanford’s graduation weekend. Even from the other side, Dad, you have fun. 

This week, my mother gave me one of my father’s most prized possessions, this UT Men’s Golf National Championship ring won by his dear friend how he had the joy of being on the fairway with for the win. I will resize it, and wear it for graduation, which is also Father’s Day.
