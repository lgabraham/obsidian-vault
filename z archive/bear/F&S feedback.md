# F&S feedback
My primary request is for the writers to use simple language where possible. Where one-syllable words will do, use these. 

I know it is likely a challenge to write interesting content. Perhaps using interesting synonyms is better for SEO.

Yet one of our primary goals is to simplify the user’s experience. I hope this comes through in our writing, too.

I think simple also implies direct. So if we are talking about rugs, I would encourage the writer to use “rugs” instead of “products”.

My secondary request is to emphasize choice where possible, rather than the deal, per se. We want to emphasize empowering the buyer more so than highlighting the cheapest “deal”. 

For example, the following snippet:

“Many times, the same products are sold at different prices across different websites. As a result, it’s possible to find a better deal elsewhere on the same rug. You just have to know where to look! We have found the best sales on Joss & Main rugs, which are arguably the cutest rugs online.”

I might rewrite as:

“The same rug is often sold at many websites at dramatically different prices. We show you your best buying options for Joss & Main rugs. You can now know the best deal on the internet. Great news, given Joss & Main boasts some of the cutest rugs online.”
