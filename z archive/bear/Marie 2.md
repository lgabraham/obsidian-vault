# Marie
Marie, 

I have been thinking of you, much, in fact. Aside from my father, his father, my aunt, my mother, and a handful of others, you may we’ll be the human with the greatest influence on my life trajectory. 

It means so much to me now. First, because the Stanford label represented pinnacle of my father’s pride in me. (Graduation is, fittingly, father’s day.) Second, because ‘family of choice’ means the world to me in the wake of his death, and this family is incalculably richer because, ultimately, of you. 

I’ve wanted very much to tell you this in person. 

Today, I met Phil Knight (Gala auction item), on the 14th anniversary of his son’s passing (of the same ill). There was a strange symmetry to his lost son and my lost father. He told me that my father would be proud, and this was an incredible testament to the poetry you enabled. 

I would be glad to see you soon. I did not expect to be in your cohort of parental loss. But maybe we have things to teach one another. 

Another classmate lost his SO shockingly the day prior. I have found comfort in a grief comrade. Perhaps Molly’s and my father’s legacy will be to encourage our class to wonder, critically, “What matters most to them, and why?”

Love to you.
