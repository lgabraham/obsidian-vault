It will be difficult to summarize their love. I will try.

Marcin and Jenn moved in with one another within one week of dating.

Now, this was a sign of the times — where hey gave each other Covid as their first gift.  Yet there is more.

Marcin teaches me how to love. 

He has taught me, now many times over, that great love always involves some element of blind faith. A leap.

I tend to want to control love — to protect myself from its pain. This is a fool’s errand, Marcin shows me a better way.

Yes, sometimes, to me at least, Marcin’s love appears as reckless abandon. But it is precisely the wholeheartedness with which he commits himself that I admire, and that I aspire to emulate.

Their relationship also began with Marcin’s hesitation and Jenn’s clear determination. Marcin would ask “are you sure?” And Jenn would respond “shut up and kiss me”.

Marcin once described Jenn to me as “an ambitious girlfriend to have”. In part, this speaks to his implicit respect. But of course he is right.

I have come to attribute to them both impossible romance. Of course, they had the entire city of New York to themselves as their playground.

Their relationship began with a kind of carefree play and support — sillily singing and surrounded by beloved friends.

It all just *worked*. And when it works, there is no need to question or second guess.

There are so many little signals. Like when Jenn spoke Russian to Babcia, English to [Marcin’s mother], and French to [Marcin’s French Aunt]. 

Once, when they were staying with Mimosa and me, they each separately and independently brought back flowers that matched Mimosa’s hair.
