# Bearing the risk of back rent
Dan,

I am putting in writing what we spoke of before with the Port of San Francisco, PPP funds, and our respective risk.

Cabrio Taxi has ~$22,000 of cash in its bank account (all PPP funds). Here is where we currently stand with the Port:

$27,000 “back rent” for space
$1,800 “back rent” for % of sales (forgiven)

The spirit of our agreement, as I understand it is as follows: All of the $22K goes towards the $27K of “back rent”. If the residual $5K is not ever forgiven, you would have to pay it over 12 months starting in June at $400/mo. This would come off of the seller’s note. 

In this case, you pay the $10K insurance, and rent ($3K) starting in April.

Alternatively, we could use the PPP funds as follows: $12k gores towards “back rent” while $10.5K towards insurance. 

In this case, $15K is at risk of not being forgiven. I am willing to take on the $5K of risk already presumed in the contract. 

The additional $10K of risk, according to my understanding of the contract would be “yours”. You would choose to take it on because you believe (and I agree) it is the best path doe the company. 

For the sake of cooperation, though, I would be willing to **split** the total risk — $7.5K a piece, i.e. $2.5K more than the contract says I “owe”.  

In other words, if the Port insists on no forgiveness (unlikely), you would pay the $1,250 for 12 months starting in June. Half or $625/mo. would come off the seller’s note. Half would be paid by you. 

Whatever you did have to pay of this, we would split. This means I am not only bearing more risk in sum total, but more risk across every outcome. 

Note that if Cabrio Taxi has to pay $8K ($20K total rent), in the first scenario, I bare none of that risk, as we would had already paid $22K in rent. In the second scenario, I bare $4K of risk. 

I’m not mother Theresa here, I just want to be clear about the economics of mine that I am “putting on the alter” for the sake of our partnership. 

And of course, you are also bearing $7.5K of risk that was not baked into our contact. The Port could demand that some or all of that is paid, and in the original contract, you wouldn’t have cared whether or not they did. 

Personally, I would consent to this arrangement because I believe this best sets the company up for success, because make overture towards the beginning of this new phase of our partnership, and because I am reasonably sure the forgiveness conversation will fall in our favor.

All in all, iIt has a nice “feel” to it. We each bear more risk than the contract states in the language, so that we can increase the chances that the company thrives. 

I’m happy to discuss this by phone as well. Yet you were right to ask for it in writing, as it is just complicated enough that we could have had miscommunications.
