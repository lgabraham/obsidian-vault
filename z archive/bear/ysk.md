# ysk

Why ysk. You can save a lot of money finding out what an item *really* is at *every* stores that sell it.
**For example,** the *exact same item* appears at 4 stores with 4 different names:
* “Haley” at Wayfair for 479
* “Raelyn” at Target for 479
* “French Country” at Amazon for 271
* “Ashley Raelyn” at Homemaker’s Furniture for 237

In this case, I would suggest the item *really* is the “Realyn” nightstand made by Ashley Furniture. If you kew this, and spent 5 minutes on Google, you can save 240.
**This practice is most prevalent in certain categories.**
My example is in furniture, which is relevant to my work. This also happens in many other categories, including mattresses, tools, generic drugs, and some electronics.
The intent of this practice is not *always* to deceive you. Sometimes sellers optimize the name of their items for keyword searches.
Still, the result is the same — if you are unaware, and don’t do your homework, you may pay more. It seems to be getting worse, not better.
**Here are generic tools for your research:**
* Google Lens to reverse image search stock images
* “dupe” category subreddits (furniture, decor, fashion, etc.) on this blessed website
* Spoken website (furniture only)
* Browser extensions like: Capital One, Rakuten, Amazon Assistant (these are hit or miss)
* I don’t have a magic bullet here. I am merely spreading awareness and some tools that can serve as defense.
