# Paul Graham

PG, you don’t know me. I am in the current YC batch as Dane’s cofounder. If I force ranked those I admire most, you would be in the top 3. 

I joined Dane in YC last summer. I was star-struck when you appeared at orientation, and when Jessica exclaimed “Dane’s here!” 

We deferred because I had my first child weeks before Demo Day. Dane was gracious enough to give me the time to fall in love with my daughter. 

I often consider the words of a father who watched you delight in play with your child without a sense of time. He marveled at this before he found out the father was you. How could you be prodigious in work and also be so present as a Dad? This inspires me.

I am now building a company that I believe will change the world alongside others who intimidate and inspire me.  So many of us are able to work on projects we enjoy — and admire — because of what your built.

Dane and I named our legal entity “Treehouses, Inc.” after your essay A Project of Own’s Own. Building this company with Dane has very much felt like building our own treehouse. 

You surely don’t invest on meaning alone. You must believe in our business. I’ll leave that convincing to Dane. I won’t admire you less if you don’t invest. I will be fueled to my capacity, though, if you do. 

Thank you for reading. 

So many minds actualized through effort enjoy and admire actualized through hard work YC you built. 


I worked with my wife on a project of ours for 8 years. Our love and effectiveness is one of my proudest achievements. You and Jessica offer model to emulate. 

The fact that you built this institution, as formidable as any I have participated in, is yet another reason I admire you.
