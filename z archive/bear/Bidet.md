# Bidet
It is (of course) relevant whether you have nearby electrical outlets or hot water lines. Pulling electrical is easier than you think, unless you have tile. I’ve never been turned off by a neat extension cord. 

For us, I bought:

(1) a Tushy Spa for our guest toilet, which has nearby hot water but tile + far away outlet. It tied to hot water. The simplicity and price is right. BUT their solution does not have a warm seat, one of the biggest pleasures of bidet-dom. 

(2) an Omigo SL for my upstairs toilet, where I had an electrician install a nearby outlet. I had come down to a Bio Bidet Prestige or Omigo SL, as both are “full stack” under $500. 

I had been leaning Bio Bidet bc I prefer controls directly on the seat, but the Omigo has a 20% that won me.

There are a bunch of $200-$300 seats (some mentioned here) that don’t seem to have a front runner for me, plus the $500+ “Cadillac” solutions (endless warm water, multiple user settings) that seem unnecessary.
