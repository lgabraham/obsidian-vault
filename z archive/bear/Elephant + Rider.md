# Elephant + Rider
 🐘 🧘 

Additions:

Dogs and humans develop stronger bonds through training. Even just the dedicated attention. 

The predominant experience I’ve had while meditating is experiencing my conscious brain as a rider on the elephant that is my full brain. 

If you were a rider in an elephant, you would never attempt to “control” the elegant. You would aim for deeper trust. You would develop compassion for when it wanted to run or for when it was startled or excited. 

And yet you also wouldn’t lose your cool when it did this, or lose your resolve, else the elephant might be “in charge”. Even when you “mastered” the elephant, if you respected it, you would never believe you had full dominion over it. You would see it as it’s own independent being. You are would also have compassion for yourself when you made a mistake. 

You also would take care of the elephant daily if you wanted the best possible relationship. You wouldn’t expect the elephant to heel right away after not seeing you for many days. Though you might also find a familiarity of the trust you had previously developed. 

And so it is with my brain(s). 

What is the elephant? It is a messy intersection of my emotional, sexual, automatic being. If I am angry or aroused (or the elephant is running), it may take some time for the elephant to slow down (begin to walk). 

While the rider “manages” the elephant, the elephant manages the rider too. When the elephant is calm, the rider may be calmer. Of course the elephant would understand if not always, at least sometimes, understand this power. 

A happy elephant makes for a happier rider and visa versa. But a happy elephant makes more for a happy rider, as he is easier to steer.
