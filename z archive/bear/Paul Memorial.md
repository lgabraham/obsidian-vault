# Paul Memorial

It is hard to put into words all that Paul taught me. 

He dazzled me with competence. My first day of class took place over an elliptical table he had hand-hewn, where he then recited two ancient poems he had translated — one in Chinese, and one in Greek. 

Later in my 20s, I would get a tattoo that Paul inspired. The word was *Arete*, which Paul might translate as moral excellence. In hindsight, I realize I committed myself so deeply to this idea, because I had watched Paul so clearly live it. He pointed the way to its beauty. 

Watching Paul may not have been enough. I also experienced his love. And for all of Paul’s excellence, perhaps he was most excellent in love. At a Plan 2 symposium on creativity, I asked him where it was most important in life to be creative. His response: “in showing the people that you love that you love them.”￼

Above all else, Paul lived *this*. I was deeply affected by his love, as a student, and as a friend. He even conducted a symposium on love at my bachelor party, which I dare to say would never have made sense to me until I met Paul. Having met him, of course, and having had him shaped who I am today, it makes perfect sense. 

For all that Paul imbued my life with — certainly inspiration for reflecting on what excellence means to me — his chief gift was love. I was, in a real sense, transformed by his love, through his teaching and friendship. For me, this will be his legacy.
