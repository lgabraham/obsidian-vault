# Furniture w meaning, past present and future 

My family immigrated from Lebanon. My grandfather started shining shoes. Working in coal mines. 

When we made it, he purchased beautiful wooden leather chairs. I used to sit in one chair, and he in the other, and he would dispense wisdom.  The chairs are now in my home office. 

Furniture can hold a lot of history, and so a lot meaning. While families move homes, they can take furniture items with them. Interestingly, furniture etymology is from “moving”

It’s not just the chairs. 

My father, by coincidence, had a furniture store. He loved wooden furniture. He particularly loved a wooden desk. The desk I write on. 

My great grandmother was a gardener. She made epic Lebanese meals. My family sits around her dining room table. 

I also have a whole storage unit full of furniture that had meaning to my family but no longer has meaning to me. I haven’t quite found the courage to let it go. 

Indeed while my grandfather was immensely proud to have stuff, and grew up in the Depression that would instill a kind of deprivation, my father and mother had too much stuff. Part of the meaning to me is to be released from it. To make space for my own stuff with its own meaning. 

Furniture is expensive and so receiving furniture from family is appealing. 

Something I’ve heard a lot from our users is the meaning of pride of buying something for themselves. Not a used item but _adult furniture_— a signal that they are secure enough in their life to invest in something that will last. 

Maybe those items will become meaningful to someone else — are children, or a friend who loves us. 

We receive meaning from our ancestors but then must make our own and shape the stories that will inform.
