# Stories
Based on what we’ve discussed, there are a few stories you might be telling yourself that could limit your sense of freedom and potential. Here are some examples, along with reframes that incorporate compassion and possibility:

**1. Story: “I am responsible for others’ happiness or stability.”**

This could stem from your role as an emotional interpreter in childhood or your deep commitment to relationships. It may lead you to feel overburdened or hesitant to prioritize your own needs.

**Reframe:**

*“I can care deeply for others without taking responsibility for their emotions. I am at my best when I honor my needs and trust others to navigate their own paths.”*
* **Compassion**: Recognize the love and care that motivates this story—it reflects your desire to connect and nurture.
* **Possibility**: Embrace the idea that by setting boundaries and prioritizing your authenticity, you can actually deepen your relationships and inspire those around you.

**2. Story: “My value lies in how well I connect with or support others.”**

Your capacity for intimacy is a strength, but it may leave you questioning whether achievements or independence are less meaningful.

**Reframe:**

*“My value lies in my full humanity—my capacity to connect, create, and grow as an individual. Balancing intimacy and independence allows me to honor all parts of myself.”*
* **Compassion**: Appreciate the beauty of your desire for deep connection—it’s a skill and gift.
* **Possibility**: Consider that cultivating independence might enhance your connections, as it allows you to bring a fuller, more grounded self to others.

**3. Story: “I need to be perfect or successful to prove my worth.”**

This could be tied to your Stanford MBA, your startup ambitions, or even the sense of responsibility you carry as a parent and partner.

**Reframe:**

*“My worth is inherent, independent of my achievements. I can embrace growth and creativity without needing them to define me.”*
* **Compassion**: Acknowledge that striving for excellence reflects your drive and care for the world around you.
* **Possibility**: Imagine a world where your efforts are fueled by joy and curiosity rather than pressure—this can lead to greater innovation and fulfillment.

**4. Story: “If I am not in control, things will fall apart.”**

This could stem from early life experiences where you felt the need to manage others’ emotions or mediate family dynamics.

**Reframe:**

*“I can trust in the resilience of others and the natural flow of life. I don’t have to carry everything alone.”*
* **Compassion**: Recognize the strength and resourcefulness that helped you manage challenging situations.
* **Possibility**: Consider that releasing control can invite creativity, collaboration, and unexpected solutions.

**5. Story: “I can’t take risks because the stakes are too high.”**

This may be tied to financial caution, your startup, or past experiences where risk didn’t pan out as you hoped (e.g., stock market).

**Reframe:**

*“I can take thoughtful risks that align with my values and vision, trusting in my ability to adapt and grow from any outcome.”*
* **Compassion**: Appreciate your instinct to protect your family and future—it reflects your care and responsibility.
* **Possibility**: Reframe risk-taking as an opportunity to create new possibilities and deepen your trust in life’s unfolding.

**How to Begin the Reframing Process**
	1.	**Notice the story**: Write it down when you hear it in your thoughts.
	2.	**Validate its origins**: Acknowledge where it comes from—your history, values, or protective instincts.
	3.	**Rewrite it with care**: Ask, “What perspective would allow me to grow while honoring my intentions?”
	4.	**Practice the reframe**: Repeat it to yourself, especially when you feel constrained by the old story.

These reframes can help you embrace both your strengths and your potential, freeing you to live more expansively and with greater self-compassion. Let me know which stories resonate most and how they feel to you.
