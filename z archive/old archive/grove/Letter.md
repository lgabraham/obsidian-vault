I hardly know what to say about 955 Grove #3, this magical hidden gem. I found my feet in San Francisco in this little home. My wife and I fell in love here, and then (during Covid) got married in the park just steps away. Dear friends later moved in as neighbors, which has been one of the home's great unexpected gifts.

The home manages to be tucked away and private while sitting in the heart of San Francisco. As someone from the Southwest, I always appreciated that it shares no walls with anyone. It feels like a real house, not an apartment. From the yard you can see the back of the Painted Ladies. This little home has all of their dignity, yet is perfectly sized for an individual or a couple.

I love the light in the mornings. Opening the patio door to hear the birds. Working in the shed as if it were my own private office, taking walking breaks around the park. The view of the City from our bedroom window, and the cool air the bedroom enjoys at night, especially after a cheeky late-night sauna.

Alamo Square Park is steps away, where we ate countless Bi-Rite sandwiches. The Financial District is a quick jaunt. Divisadero, Japantown, the Haight, and Hayes Valley are all walkable, and we have several handfuls of favorite restaurants within a few blocks.

Since we bought it, the home went from a former horse barn that still very much felt like a horse barn into a truly dignified little home, with a second story you can stand in and many modern comforts. 

We've loved every season here. We're only moving on because our family has grown (now with two kids) and the next chapter calls for more room. We hope whoever lands here delights in its San Francisco charm, finds the same wonderful neighbors, and feels, as we did, like they've stumbled into magic and romance just right for this moment of life.
