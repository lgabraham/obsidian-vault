# Emotions w/ Parents
People with anxious-avoidant attachment style may struggle to engage with their parents (or any close figures) due to their internal conflict between desiring closeness and fearing it. This can manifest in several ways:

1. Fear of Rejection: They may fear rejection or abandonment, so they avoid getting too close to their parents to protect themselves from potential hurt.

2. Lack of Trust: They may find it hard to trust their parents due to inconsistent or unreliable care in the past. This could lead to difficulty in opening up or seeking support.

3. Difficulty Expressing Needs: They often struggle to express their needs or emotions effectively, making communication challenging. This could be due to a history of their needs being ignored or dismissed.

4. Emotional Regulation: They may have trouble regulating their emotions, resulting in feelings of anxiety or fear overwhelming their ability to engage in a meaningful relationship.

5. Fear of Vulnerability: They tend to fear vulnerability, which is a necessary component of a close relationship. This can make it difficult to connect on a deeper level.

These challenges aren't insurmountable, though. Therapy and supportive relationships can help someone with an anxious-avoidant attachment style develop healthier patterns of relating to others.
