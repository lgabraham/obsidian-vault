
> Japanese-style steamed clams in sake, mirin, and butter. Quick, fragrant, and on the table in 25 minutes.

**Serves:** 4 | **Prep:** 10 min | **Cook:** 10 min

## Ingredients
- 1¼ lb clams in shells, scrubbed
- 4 tsp sake
- 4 tsp mirin (Japanese sweet wine)
- 2 tsp rice wine vinegar
- 3 tbsp butter
- 1 green onion, chopped
- 1 tsp soy sauce

## Instructions
1. Scrub and rinse the clams. Soak in a large bowl of cold water for 5 minutes, then drain thoroughly.
2. Heat a wok or large saucepan over high heat. Quickly add the sake, mirin, and rice wine vinegar.
3. Add the clams, cover, and cook until they open, 3–4 minutes. Discard any that don't open.
4. Skim off any scum with a spoon or paper towel. Stir in the butter, green onion, and soy sauce, tossing to coat until the butter melts.
5. Arrange the clams on a plate, drizzle the sauce over the top, and serve immediately.

## Notes
- Runs on the sweet side. For a clearer broth, skip the soy sauce and season with a little kosher salt instead.
- Easy to scale up — if you double the clams, you don't need to fully double the butter.
- Be careful not to overcook, or the clams turn chewy.
- Good as a main or a side.
