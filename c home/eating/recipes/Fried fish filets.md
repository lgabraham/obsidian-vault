
> Crispy pan-fried fish with a golden crust and juicy inside. Done in about 5 minutes — the trick is a hot heavy pan and not moving the fish until it releases.

**Serves:** 2 | **Prep:** 3 min | **Cook:** 5 min

## Ingredients
- 2 thin fish fillets (~5 oz / 150g each, about 1 cm thick), skin on or off
- Salt and pepper
- ¼ cup (35g) plain flour
- 1 tsp paprika (optional)
- 2–3 tbsp oil (vegetable or canola), enough to thinly cover the pan
- Lemon wedges, to serve
- Chopped parsley or dill, to garnish (optional)

## Instructions
1. Pat the fish completely dry on both sides with paper towels (especially important for thawed frozen fish).
2. Season both sides well with salt and pepper.
3. Mix the flour and paprika on a plate. Coat the fish on both sides, pressing firmly so it sticks, then shake off the excess well.
4. Heat a heavy skillet over medium-high until you see wisps of smoke. Add the oil and swirl to coat — it heats in seconds.
5. Add the fish; it should sizzle immediately. Cook 2 minutes until golden and crisp, pressing down gently, then flip and cook the other side 2 minutes.
6. If it sticks, don't force it — it releases naturally once golden. If it's browning too fast, lift the pan off the heat briefly.
7. Serve immediately with lemon wedges and a sprinkle of herbs. It loses crispiness after about 5 minutes, so eat it fresh.

## Notes
- Use a heavy pan (cast iron or good non-stick). Heating the pan *before* adding oil is the key to no sticking.
- Shaking off excess flour matters — too much flour burns and the crust falls off when flipped.
- Any fish fillet works; thinner fillets give the best crisp-to-flesh ratio.
- Simple lemon butter sauce: melt 3 tbsp salted butter, stir in 1½ tbsp fresh lemon juice.
