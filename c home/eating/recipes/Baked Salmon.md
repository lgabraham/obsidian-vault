
> Easy weeknight baked salmon with a simple brown sugar spice rub. Tender, flaky, and done in 20 minutes.

**Serves:** 6 | **Prep:** 5 min | **Cook:** 15 min

## Ingredients
- 1 tbsp brown sugar
- 1½ tsp kosher salt
- 1½ tsp sweet paprika
- 1½ tsp garlic powder
- ½ tsp freshly cracked black pepper
- 1 (2-pound) salmon fillet
- 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil

## Instructions
1. Preheat the oven to 375°F with a rack in the center position.
2. In a small bowl, combine the brown sugar, salt, paprika, garlic powder, and pepper.
3. Place the salmon on a foil-lined rimmed baking sheet, skin side down. Brush with olive oil, then rub evenly with the spice mixture until completely coated.
4. Bake for 12–15 minutes (depending on thickness), or until the fish flakes easily with a fork. Internal temp: 125°F for medium, up to 140°F for well-done.

## Notes
- Line the baking sheet with foil — salmon skin sticks badly to an unlined pan.
- Many reviewers cut the salt to 1 tsp; worth trying if you're salt-sensitive.
- Honey can substitute for the brown sugar in equal amounts.
- Leftovers keep 3 days in the fridge; best eaten cold (on salad, in pasta, or as salmon eggs benedict). Doesn't reheat or freeze well.
