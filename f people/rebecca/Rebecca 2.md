# Rebecca

Rebecca, Mimosa mentioned to me y’all attended a class on how to speak to your children about your finances if they are resistant. My dear love claimed she was not resistant, then went on to very much describe being resistant. We both love her, dearly, I know. 

I understand why one would resist thinking about their parent’s death. I resist thinking about your death too, because I love you, and the thought of you not being with us is terribly painful. 

I also understand the peace of mind elders seek in explaining to their children where all the documents and piles are. I already feel it in myself. I have too many passwords, accounts, tax bills, etc. and I work very hard to make sure they are organized. They are the sum of my work, savings, etc. to date. 

If and when a time comes when no one will be around to communicate the contents of “the folder” to Mimosa, then she will be in deep grief, and the folder will not be foremost on her mind. It may even feel like an odious distraction from honoring your absence. She loves me in so many ways. One way I may love her well is by taking on the folder’s matters. 

Which is to say, that if you should want to talk with someone from our family about the contents of your folder, I am willing, and I want you to know this. 

I do recognize that it is important that you address the contents with your children directly. I also honor that they are the benefactors, the deciders, etc. of your stuff. 

What I have that may be valuable is: experience with the all the relevant matters, a capacity to be effective in emotional times, a personal motivation that the intersection of administrative items and death these be handled in a sane and responsible way, and a love of the children you raised. 

I hope this gives you some peace.

Love, your son-in-love-and-law, Geoff
