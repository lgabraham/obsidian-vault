# Dhruv

Why we failed

What happened? Post ingestion felt terrible. We didn’t trust the data or they process. 

Burning Man was a checkpoint for us — did we believe we were in the right path? We concluded we have a good product vision and poor process to operational use. 

Why? We dug. At the data schema level, our site data was misaligned with the data as we have it in such a way that ingestion didn’t scale without error. 

On the one hand, this was our strategic error. If we knew what we know now, we would have been far more proactive in the data schema design. 

We took inventory off our values: agency, momentum, excellence, play, courage, accountability. Worst then failing on our goals, we did not believe we were living up to these values. 

Submitting an issue is not agency. Create sometimes accelerated momentum, and sometimes stalled it, but we did not have full accountability over why. 

In August, after Dane’s trip to Iceland, we played with building the data flows from the ground up. It was fun. Most importantly, all of our values were expressed. 

To me, and to us, this meant we needed to do more of what we are doing, and less of what we were doing. 

Why we failed:

We expect 1s percentile speed, attention as default dead
We feel delays lost context grievously as involved founders
Context is a multiplier on small teams
Our priorities change abruptly and ruthlessly as PMF seekers

This does hurt like a cofounder that did not work out. I also have reflected on our journey for some time, and believe it is the best and kindest next step. 

Lastly we didn’t fail. Y’all got us to where we are and we couldn’t be here without you. I am immensely grateful for that.
