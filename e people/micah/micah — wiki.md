**Snapshot.** My first dear friend made outside of school — we truly chose each other. A poet's heart, a philosopher's mind, and a dark comic's sense of humor. We're both only children, and we found brotherhood in one another. The friend I can laugh anywhere with, until we cry.

## History & arc
We met pedicabbing in San Francisco; the interview dissolved into conversation and we've had the irresistible ease of two kids at play ever since — "more rightly playing nerdy dragon games in a park."

The relationship is dense with shared adventures: the **AIDS/LifeCycle** SF-to-LA bike ride (Micah always in a helmet amid a sea of 500 bare heads), **Burning Man**, endless nights of board games, and a friendship so integrated into my life that I invited him on our **honeymoon** — to Mimosa's surprise.

Then came **Tatiana.** When I first heard of her I felt protective — only children don't love sharing their one friend. But I formed my own relationship with her, came to treasure her, and watched Micah become better for her love. I **officiated their wedding** and gave the toast ("To Micah and Tatiana"). I'd earlier organized his **bachelor party** in NYC — Moxy Times Square, a roast, sushi, the Comedy Cellar — a "love bath" I won't soon forget him beaming through.

Now there's **Athena**, their daughter, and a new dimension: watching Micah commit to bringing books into **Naya's** life made me realize there are still more sides of our friendship to uncover.

## Candid reflections
The deepest current here is the only-child dynamic. We share so well *because* we're only children — and that's exactly why Tatiana arriving was hard. "We are sometimes in a fight for his heart." But I've come to believe her opinions are often quite good, that she loves him fiercely and defends him, and that him listening to her over me is the right kind of growth. It is one of the greatest gifts of my life that two only children found brotherhood in one another.

Micah makes me feel funny — "I used to think I was really funny, then I realized Micah is just really good at making me feel really funny." Mimosa has had to remind me, when I float a joke too far, "I'm not Micah."

There's a tender, unspoken project in how I think about him: identity *vis-à-vis* Seaside. My coaching gift was deliberate — I "really believe a coaching relationship will transform your identity vis-à-vis Seaside." The "Endurance / Shackleton" riffs (privilege, the elements, chatting up women in the Seaside bookstore) are affectionate ribbing pointed at something real.
