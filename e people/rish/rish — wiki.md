## History & arc
We were **GSB classmates** but barely close then; the friendship caught fire around **2023–2024** (the bulk of our 3,200+ WhatsApp messages cluster there). Early on it was him pulling me into his world — **Robot Heart** parties (Burning Man camp), a **marathon** he was training for in late 2023 (he was chasing sub-3; "you coming is all the motivation I need" — though injury meant it "just didn't come together"). By Feb 2024 he could write me: *"I love you so so so deeply my friend — I couldn't have imagined that when we graduated GSB… the depth it could reach in friendship."*

He's also woven into my family — protective and present about **Naya** ("you being there for Naya… is way more important to me").

The friendship runs deep enough that we write each other long, candid letters — the kind that try to name a person's superpower and blind spot to their face.

A pivotal turn: Rish asked me to **coach** him. I was hesitant — "the prospect intimidates me, because I respect you so" — and above all I didn't want to jeopardize the friendship. After sleeping on it, I realized coaching "lacks a mutuality and a shared win condition," and what I actually wanted was to *build something with him*. That became **The Explorer Fund** — Rish, me, Marcin, and Andrew ("the 4 of us"), where I'd play wrangler/PM, bring a pre-GSB and YC network, and do founder coaching where useful. I committed to raising my own $1M in three months and seeding it with my own capital. I've also become his **editor** — reworking his fund memos and announcements to "unleash your most effective voice, not make your voice my own." Sharvil came in as an LP; I introduced the two of them.

## Candid reflections
My core read of Rish is two states. **"In love"** — with the future, an idea, a friend, a lover, a moment — when the power of his emotion illuminates the genuinely incredible machinery of his mind, and he's magnetic. And **"mania"** — audacious declarations untethered from reality, high emotion and activity while out of touch with his own commitments. The difference, I think, is whether he's in touch with his truth. (I know there's a lot of *me* in that read, and I hold it with love.)

The word I keep landing on is **restless** — an abiding refusal to be satisfied that is both a superpower and the thing I worry about. I worry the restlessness will deny him the rest he needs to stay young in body and mind, and that whatever animates it could keep him from a grounding center with a committed partner. My recurring wish for him: that he offer himself the same empathy he so readily offers others — without losing the drive.

The "hard truths" I've put to him: personal meaning is bound up with constraint; partnership requires sacrifice and the only variable is willingness; secure love lets the subconscious thrive (he may underestimate this); fear of commitment is an invitation to understand himself; boundless freedom risks becoming shallow. None of it prescribes a decision — I love and support him whatever path he takes.

What surprised me most in knowing him better: how **others-centric** he is. Powerfully empathetic, attuned to how each person arrives at psychological safety — visible in his leadership and the art of his "soft asks." His principles govern him.

**Where we differ:** Rish is unabashedly Randian — merit over equality, suspicious of "wokeness," moved by *Atlas Shrugged*'s idea that the capable must carry the world ("technology acceleration is the only path forward"). I come at things from therapy, rhythm, and a more progressive instinct. We argue these openly and it never threatens the bond — if anything the contrast is part of the fun. (Worth holding lightly and without judgment in how I engage him.)
