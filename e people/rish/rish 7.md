# rish 
“Be satisfied with what you have”

A teacher told me this when I was 8 years old, and it made me angry. It contradicted something my parents taught me — to be curious and seek the new. 

This would set the stage for an battle of values that would define my internal monologue for years to come. One voice spoke the values of my parents — of independence, self improvement, and pursuing big dreams. The other spoke in the voice of my wider culture — of collectivism, tradition, and seeking certainty. 

I won’t bore you with my entire life-story, but this struggle of external and internal value systems shaped me. It wasn’t until I moved to the USA that I felt like I had found home, and my internal and external world found harmony.

“I will never be satisfied”

These words from Lin Manuel’s Hamilton rang the year I moved to the USA (2015). It has been quite a journey since, with many ups and downs.

Last month marked a milestone for me, as I, newly American, saw the name of the company Tanuj, Sud and I built with the help of an amazing team and investors splashed across the Nasdaq. I am reminded to never to be satisfied. 

Ten years from now, the idea that businesses with physical operations depend on cameras as intelligent sensors to run and understand their operations will seem obvious in hindsight. I am extremely excited about what we will build at Spot AI and to bring the promise of video intelligence to businesses across the globe. We will strive to have the Spot ticker up and running.
