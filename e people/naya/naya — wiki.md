## History & arc
She was flying before she could walk — **first flight at 8 weeks**, Southwest #945 OAK→AUS, Mimosa and I nervous, the crew (Emily, Mona, Karyn) spoiling her with a certificate and pin. "Given her parents, Naya would likely always be a traveler."

The years since are a reel of small, vivid scenes: at **3**, "what a blessing, what a joy"; the afternoon she double-fisted two squares of chocolate, queued up Satori on my phone, pulled on a tie-dye shirt, and "self-guided into a sugar-high rave about 30 years before I did." At **4**, she told me: *"I couldn't sleep because I was busy dreaming with my eyes open."* Her stories tumble out — lamb head, dancing beat drops, ball and basket, tattoos ("like this one, not this one").

## Reflections (as her father)
She is, to me, the greatest responsibility I've been entrusted with — and pouring love into her alongside Mimosa is its own happy project; I have so much love to give, and where better to place it.

A note I keep for myself: **don't look for validation from Naya — share your feelings *with* her** instead. And on what to carry for her: "there are things she can see I can show her, things she can see I can't show her, and things she doesn't need to know" — *trusting Naya*, and trusting myself on the line between them. She has also **witnessed my grief**, which I want to hold consciously — letting her see real feeling without making her carry it.
