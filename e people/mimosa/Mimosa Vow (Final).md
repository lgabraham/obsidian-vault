# Mimosa Vow (Final)
My life is full of more blessings than I could have ever imagined. You are the most important blessing of them all. 

Sometimes I think we must have stumbled into a cheat code. We have too much fun, know too many adventures, have too much fortune in these friends. 

I still remember the first time we hugged. It is as if my body knew that you would have the most loving pets. You do. To this day, touch is our most primary and consistent way to speak. 

I soon realized that I respected you more than any other woman I had ever met. It was your solidity. The many facets of your mind. Your courage. Your ease. Your presence in other’s lives. 9 years in, and my awe of you only grows each year. I still don’t understand how this is possible. 

We discovered that together we can move mountains and undertake great journeys. Our shared life is still my grandest adventure. We learned that we enjoy enveloping those we love in the warmth we have to offer. 

Of all of our strengths, though, perhaps my favorite is our ability to bark and babble endlessly about nothing at all, often giggling all the while. 

Every year our relationship is not only the single greatest source of meaning, pride, and love in my life, but also of my own growth. It is as though it has taken me 9 years to fully appreciate you and us, and I am not nearly done. 
**Mimosa:** You are my glue. All of the love in my life, with all those I love, is strengthened by your mere presence. 
You are my teacher. You model for me — and I think all of us around you — to love more generously, more reliably, and more fiercely. 
You are my anchor. You keep me grounded in what matters. 
You are my pillar. You stand on your own. You also, occasionally, prop me up. 
You are my playmate. You are game. For 9 years, we have had nonstop fun. 
You are my solace. As I have accepted myself, you too have accepted all of me. 
You are a perfect object of my affection. 
More than any other place in this world, you are my home. 
Your magic is that you do all of this, it seems, simply by being yourself.
**Now, to what I vow to you:** I choose you as my forever playmate. 
I embrace you in all your power and all your weakness.
I vow to build with you the most robust, technicolor model of love with you of which I am capable. 
I vow at once to challenge you to grow and to love you precisely where you are. 
I vow to honor your truth even when it is not my own. 
I promise to be aloof, sometimes confusingly solitary, sometimes so steeped in my feelings, sometimes not at all resilient to conflict, or even to the smallest changes in what we’re having for dinner. 
I vow to have patience with your piles, to not correct you when you say left when you mean right, and to not expect you to feel your feelings on any timeline other than your own.
I vow to nurture a happy nest for our family, Naya, and our tribe, 
I vow to deepen our sense of home together, even and especially when no one else is present.
